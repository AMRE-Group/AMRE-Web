Most LA duplexes that hit the market are two stacked boxes with new countertops. This one isn't. 6532 Commodore Sloat is a 1927 Spanish Revival in Carthay Circle — one of the city's protected Historic Preservation Overlay Zones — with the kind of period detailing that literally cannot be replicated under HPOZ rules: cathedral-style cove ceilings, arched doorways, faux fireplaces, step-up formal dining rooms, French doors opening to private patios. The previous owner did extensive renovations, the roof was just replaced, and right now both units are vacant.

It went back on market this month after a brief detour into backup-offers status. So the numbers, finally, are worth running.

The Numbers

The listing publishes its income projections directly, which is uncommon for small multifamily at this price point. Per the MLS, each two-bedroom unit projects at $5,000/mo — aggressive but not implausible for a renovated Spanish Revival inside the Carthay Circle HPOZ, walking distance to LACMA and The Grove.

A 4.0% cap and 17.5× GRM aren't going to win any cash-flow contests on a spreadsheet. But this isn't a deal pitched on yield — it's pitched on location, architecture, and optionality. You're paying for the HPOZ premium, the period detailing you can't reproduce, and the fact that you set both rents on day one with no inherited tenants absorbing.

This is where the math gets interesting. A single-family of comparable size and quality in Carthay Circle runs well into the high $2Ms or low $3Ms. Buy this as a duplex at $1.995M, live in one unit, and rent the other for $5,000/mo — you're effectively living in a Carthay Circle Spanish Revival at roughly the carrying cost of a much smaller home elsewhere. That's the trade.

Why it works

  • ✓Both units delivered vacant — you set the rents from day one. No inherited leases, no rent-controlled tenancies absorbing future upside.
  • ✓Period detailing intact — cove ceilings, arched doorways, faux fireplaces, step-up dining rooms with French doors. The kind of plasterwork artisans spend weeks reproducing, here on day one.
  • ✓Renovated kitchens + en-suite baths — granite counters, remodeled kitchens and bathrooms, central HVAC, stacked laundry in-unit on both sides.
  • ✓Brand-new roof — replaced last year per the listing. A meaningful capex line item already off the table.
  • ✓Two garages + gated driveway — four total parking spaces, gated entry. Real value in this corridor.
  • ✓Separate fenced yards + mature fruit trees — not a shared slab. Each unit has its own private outdoor space, which renters in this market pay up for.

Location

  • —LACMA, the Petersen, and the Academy Museum are walking-radius — world-class culture on a daily commute.
  • —The Grove + Original Farmers Market minutes away — LA's most-trafficked retail and dining hub.
  • —Cedars-Sinai nearby — a massive year-round employer drawing high-earning renters.
  • —Purple Metro Line extension through Beverly Hills coming online — transit access in this corridor is about to improve.
  • —Beverly Grove dining + Little Ethiopia walkable. Carthay Center Elementary (7/10 GreatSchools) is 0.2 miles away.

⚠

Know before you go. Carthay Circle is a Historic Preservation Overlay Zone. HPOZ protects neighborhood architectural character (great for long-term value) but restricts exterior alterations and may affect interior renovations. Buyers and agents should independently verify all HPOZ restrictions, bed/bath count, sq ft, and permitting directly with the City of Los Angeles. The property is also subject to LA's rent control framework once tenanted.

Quick answers

How much is the duplex at 6532 Commodore Sloat Dr?
It's listed at $1,995,000 — a 1927 Spanish Revival duplex in Carthay Circle, Los Angeles (90048), delivered fully vacant. MLS #26805127, listed by Sotheby's International Realty.
How much rent can the units generate?
Each two-bedroom unit is projected at $5,000/mo per the MLS — $10,000/mo, or $114,000/yr, gross across both. After roughly $34,200 of estimated expenses, projected NOI is approximately $79,800.
What's the cap rate and GRM?
Per the MLS, the projected cap rate is 4.00% and the gross rent multiplier is approximately 17.5×, based on $114,000 projected gross income against the $1,995,000 list price.
Is it in a historic district?
Yes — Carthay Circle is a Historic Preservation Overlay Zone (HPOZ). The designation protects architectural character but places restrictions on exterior alterations. Verify all HPOZ rules with the City of Los Angeles before relying on any renovation plan.
Can you use it as a single-family home?
The listing explicitly states the approximately 2,500 sq ft of total living area could also work as a single-family residence. Conversion would be subject to LAR2 zoning, HPOZ restrictions, and local permitting — verify with the City and a qualified architect before relying on this use.
Are both units delivered vacant?
Yes — both units are vacant, so an owner sets rents from day one with no inherited leases or in-place rent-controlled tenancies.
