Almost nothing under a million dollars in Los Angeles comes delivered fully vacant, fully renovated, with two separate private yards. So a side-by-side Spanish duplex on a quiet block in Park Hills Heights is worth a closer look. Both units are 2-bed, 1-bath, both renovated, both with stainless appliances, in-unit laundry, and central HVAC — and both empty, which in this rental market is a gift.

The Numbers

At an ask of $949,000, this isn't a screaming bargain by national standards. But for an off-the-shelf duplex delivered vacant and renovated — where you control the rents from day one — the math is worth running. Estimated market rent for renovated 2-beds in this corridor is about $2,600 a month.

Operate it as a pure rental and you're looking at a tight but workable cash-flow picture once expenses hit. House-hack it — live in one unit, collect $2,600 from the other — and your effective housing cost drops well below what a single-family home in this neighborhood would run. That's been the play on duplexes like this for a decade, and it still holds.

Why it works

  • ✓Delivered 100% vacant — you set the rents. No inherited leases, no below-market tenants, no drama.
  • ✓Fully remodeled — renovated kitchens and baths, stainless appliances. Turnkey from day one.
  • ✓In-unit W/D + central HVAC in both units — the amenities that command top-of-market rent.
  • ✓A private fenced yard per unit — not a shared slab. Real outdoor space LA renters pay up for.
  • ✓LAR2 zoning — flexibility and future development upside on a 6,749 sf lot.

Quick answers

How much is the duplex at 4623 6th Avenue?
It's listed at $949,000 — a two-unit Spanish duplex in Park Hills Heights, Los Angeles (90043), delivered fully vacant and renovated.
How much rent can the units generate?
Each renovated two-bedroom unit is estimated to rent for about $2,600/month — roughly $5,200/month, or $62,400/year, gross across both units.
What is the gross rent multiplier (GRM)?
At $949,000 with about $62,400 in estimated gross annual rent, the GRM is roughly 15.2× — typical for renovated, vacant small multifamily in this part of LA.
Can you house-hack this property?
Yes. Live in one unit and rent the other for about $2,600/month to offset most of the mortgage — a common owner-occupant strategy on LA duplexes.
Is it delivered vacant?
Yes — both units are delivered vacant and renovated, so an owner sets market rents from day one with no inherited leases or rent-control tenancy to absorb.
