A walkable Westside pocket, a brand-new home, and zero of the compromises buyers usually accept.

03
Pricing Analysis · Higuera Homes

Positioned with the new-build advantage — and the right comp set.

An honest read: these are two homes on one lot — a small-lot subdivision, not detached single-family residences on separate parcels. So we anchor below the fee-simple SFR ceiling, then add back the premium new construction earns. The result is a confident, defensible band.

The Floor
Resale parity

We don't price below comparable resale homes — buyers get a brand-new home for what tired stock is fetching. That's the value hook.

The Adjustment
Small-lot

Two-on-a-lot trades at a measured discount to a detached SFR on its own parcel. We price into that reality, not against it.

The Premium
New build

Modern systems, warranty-fresh, zero deferred maintenance, designer finishes — all command a real premium over the 1920s–50s field.

How We Set The Number

We'd run a quiet off-market test before committing to a public price — letting real buyer feedback set the figure rather than a guess. Pricing two distinct floor plans also lets us position each home to its own buyer, which protects value across the pair. The exact list numbers get locked once we walk the finished product together and read the off-market response.

The Compass 3-Phase Strategy

Every property runs the same engine.

We never simply "put it on the market." Each asset is pressure-tested quietly, opened to a controlled audience, then taken wide only once demand and price are proven.

01
Phase 01

Off-Market

Quiet demand test. Agent-to-agent and database outreach. We read real buyer appetite and pricing tolerance with no public footprint and zero days-on-market accruing.

02
Phase 02

Private Exclusive

Controlled exposure. Listed inside the Compass network — Coldwell Banker, Sotheby's, Corcoran — without hitting public portals. Builds urgency among serious buyers.

03
Phase 03

On-Market

Full public launch. A polished debut backed by everything we learned in Phases 01–02. We launch from strength, not from a guess — only if the earlier phases haven't already delivered the buyer.

Why A Portfolio, Not Three Listings

The buyer pool compounds.

One at a time

Every launch starts cold. Three separate marketing pushes, three separate negotiations, and homes that may inadvertently compete with one another on price and timing.

As a coordinated portfolio

Every warm buyer carries forward. A family that loses on Schaefer is the perfect lead for Higuera. The runner-up on Unit A is the buyer for Unit B. One campaign, compounding demand — the difference between three transactions and a single, orchestrated event.
