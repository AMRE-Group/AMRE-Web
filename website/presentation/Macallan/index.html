I — Overture

A meeting two centuries in the making.

In 1824, Alexander Reid distilled the first cask of The Macallan in a stone outbuilding on the banks of the Spey. Two hundred years later — in celebration of that bicentenary — The Macallan opened its only invitation-only brand home in the United States, tucked discreetly into the hills above Bel-Air.

AMRE Real Estate Group operates from Compass Beverly Hills, eight miles to the south. Our principal is a licensed architect and licensed real estate broker — a combination that does not appear on any other team at the top of the LA market. The clients we serve are the clients The Macallan Villa was conceived to receive.

This document proposes the convening point.

An architecture of restraint.

Sleek concrete overhangs, full-height glazing, a rooftop terrace with the city spread beneath it. The interior is light, breezy, minimal — the kind of design language that lets the whisky, the food, and the conversation lead. The branded infinity pool, inscribed with the Macallan M, sits at the property's edge and reads less as ornament than as signature.

— A villa designed to disappear and let its guests be seen.

The Harmony Collection at center.

Accentuated throughout the Villa is the Harmony Collection Amber Meadow, created in partnership with Stella & Mary McCartney — inspired by the sisters' memories of the Scottish countryside. Signature artwork from David Carson, drawn from the Colour Collection, punctuates the rooms.

— Curiosity, respect, and admiration for nature — three things AMRE recognizes well.

A menu by Chef Chris Sayegh.

Chef Chris Sayegh oversees the culinary program — a series of menus that hold to Macallan's Scottish roots while drawing on sustainable Southern California ingredients. Six signature cocktails, each pulling from the Speyside estate and the SoCal terroir, complete the bar.

— Hospitality that earns the room before the whisky is poured.

A whisky concierge, by appointment.

From distinguished signature ranges to limited-release expressions, the Villa keeps a dedicated whisky concierge to curate each guest's experience — tea service, history journey, tasting, and, where appropriate, exclusive bottlings rarely seen outside Speyside.

— Every guest leaves having tasted something they cannot buy.

VII — The Partnership
Four Ways We Convene

Beyond the evening,
an ongoing arrangement.

i.

The Private Dinners.

Two AMRE-curated dinners per year at the Villa — twelve to twenty guests each — drawn from our private-client list, our Compass Beverly Hills broker network, and select members of our referral circle (architects, wealth managers, entertainment counsel).

Macallan receives: Direct hospitality access to roughly forty highly qualified guests per year — exactly the cohort the Villa was built to serve.
ii.

The Closing Gift program.

A signature Macallan expression presented at the close of each AMRE listing-side transaction — a tasting note from Michael, the bottle, and a paired invitation to the next Villa evening. AMRE handles the logistics; Macallan supplies the bottle and the invitation.

Macallan receives: Recurring placement of the brand at the highest-emotion moment of a multi-million-dollar transaction — a moment the recipient will remember for years.
iii.

The Broker Salon.

One annual evening at the Villa for the top thirty agents at Compass Beverly Hills — hosted with AMRE — featuring a tasting, dinner, and a brief on the architectural properties currently in the AMRE pipeline. A trade gathering, dressed as hospitality.

Macallan receives: Direct visibility to the brokers who themselves serve the brand's most desirable consumer cohort — multiplier effect, by design.
iv.

The Content Moments.

Long-form architectural film at AMRE's named-architect properties — Schindler, Neutra, Lautner — with Macallan integrated as the evening's pour. Editorial, not advertorial. Co-distributed across AMRE's channels, Compass's national feed, and Macallan's content partners.

Macallan receives: Brand alignment with the most editorially serious architectural content currently produced in the LA market.
