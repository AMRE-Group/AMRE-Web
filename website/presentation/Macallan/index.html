I — The Evening

An evening two centuries in the making.

To mark The Macallan's 200th anniversary, the distillery opened its only United States brand home — The Macallan Villa — in the hills above Bel-Air. The doors are kept closed except by invitation.

For one evening, those doors open for an intimate gathering hosted in partnership with Compass. A curated room. The Villa's full program. One night.

The Macallan Villa

An 8,500-square-foot brand home,
built for invitation only.

Sleek concrete overhangs. Walls of glass. A branded infinity pool inscribed with the letter M, suspended over the city below. The Villa was designed to host private clients, strategic partners, content creators, and Society members — the audience that lives within fifteen minutes of its front gate.

An architecture of restraint.

Sleek concrete overhangs, full-height glazing, a rooftop terrace with the city spread beneath it. The interior is light, breezy, minimal — the kind of design language that lets the whisky, the food, and the conversation lead. The branded infinity pool, inscribed with the Macallan M, sits at the property's edge and reads less as ornament than as signature.

— A villa designed to disappear and let its guests be seen.

The Harmony Collection at center.

Accentuated throughout the Villa is the Harmony Collection Amber Meadow, created in partnership with Stella & Mary McCartney — inspired by the sisters' memories of the Scottish countryside. Signature artwork from David Carson, drawn from the Colour Collection, punctuates the rooms.

— Curiosity, respect, and admiration for nature.

A menu by Chef Chris Sayegh.

Chef Chris Sayegh oversees the culinary program — a series of menus that hold to Macallan's Scottish roots while drawing on sustainable Southern California ingredients. Six signature cocktails, each pulling from the Speyside estate and the SoCal terroir, complete the bar.

— Hospitality that earns the room before the whisky is poured.

A whisky concierge, by appointment.

From distinguished signature ranges to limited-release expressions, the Villa keeps a dedicated whisky concierge to curate each guest's experience — tea service, history journey, tasting, and, where appropriate, exclusive bottlings rarely seen outside Speyside.

— Every guest leaves having tasted something they cannot buy.

III — The Room
The Guest List

Twenty seats. Carefully chosen.

The Villa's own audience profile reads as private clients, strategic partners, content creators, Macallan Society members, and friends and family of the brand. Compass brings the room.

The guest list draws from Compass principals, their private clients, and a small circle of culture-makers from the Westside — architects, designers, family-office principals, entertainment counsel, and founders from across the city's creative industries.

The seats are limited to twenty for the evening — a deliberate constraint to keep the room intimate and the conversation real.

IV — The Program
An Evening at the Villa

A six-act evening,
drawn from the Villa's own program.

Tea service. A two-century sensory journey. A menu by Chef Sayegh. The Harmony Collection at center. Six signature cocktails. A closing pour by the concierge.

5:30 pm

A Curated Approach

Guests are received at a discreet starting point on Sunset and shuttled in private vehicles along a curated route through Bel-Air, ending at the Villa's front gate — an arrival, not a transit.

6:30 pm

Arrival & Tea Service

Guests are received at the Villa with a tea service in the manner already established as the Villa's opening ritual — a deliberate handoff from Speyside to Southern California.

7:00 pm

A Journey Through Two Centuries

The Villa's whisky concierge leads a brief, sensory walk through The Macallan's heritage — distilled to twenty minutes — anchored by the Harmony Collection Amber Meadow by Stella & Mary McCartney.

The Villa's signature program, delivered as designed.
7:30 pm

Dinner by Chef Chris Sayegh

A five-course menu drawn from the Villa's existing culinary program — Scottish tradition through a Southern California lens — served against the David Carson Colour Collection artwork on view.

9:00 pm

The Six Cocktails

The Villa's six signature cocktails — each drawing from the Speyside estate and the Southern California terroir — served at the rooftop bar with the city below. Music kept low. Conversation kept high.

10:00 pm

A Private Pour

A closing tasting of a limited-release expression rarely poured outside Speyside, hosted by the Villa's whisky concierge. Each guest leaves with the bottle's tasting card, signed.

The room remembers the night by the last glass.
