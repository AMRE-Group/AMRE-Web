The Architect-Agent Difference

Michael Abraham is one of the only people in Los Angeles who holds both a California architecture license and a California real estate license — and the combination is structurally consequential, not just a credential. On a tour, that means a read most agents simply can't give: which walls are load-bearing, whether a dated kitchen is a weekend refresh or a six-figure undertaking, what the zoning and lot would actually allow, and where a home's real upside is hiding beneath the surface. In a city defined by architecture — from mid-century hillside modernism to character Spanish to ground-up contemporaries — that structural fluency turns every showing into a feasibility study.

Michael founded AMRE Real Estate Group at Compass with a single conviction: that the highest-quality real estate advisory in Los Angeles shouldn't require choosing between design intelligence and market expertise. At AMRE, you get both.

Focus & Experience

Michael specializes in luxury and architectural homes, value-add investment properties, and 1031-exchange strategy across Beverly Hills, Bel Air, Brentwood, Santa Monica, Malibu, Studio City, Culver City, West Adams, and the wider LA metro. With over a decade investing personally in Los Angeles multifamily real estate, he brings an owner's perspective to every transaction — not just an agent's.

His architectural training means he evaluates every property the way a developer would: light, scale, circulation, structural integrity, renovation potential, and long-term hold value, not just the current list price. For sellers, that translates to sharper positioning and more compelling marketing. For buyers, it means protection from expensive surprises and a clearer view of what a property could become.
