A perfect afternoon in Venice.

Start with a coffee on Abbot Kinney and end with dinner where the neighborhood's most celebrated kitchen just reopened. Here's how to spend a golden-hour afternoon the way locals do — on foot, from the boulevard to the beach to the canals.

The Idea

One neighborhood, on foot.

Few places in Los Angeles reward walking the way Venice does. In a single afternoon you can move from a design-forward coffee bar to three miles of oceanfront, watch the city's most democratic public stage in motion, and slip into the quiet of century-old canals — all before a dinner worth planning around. Leave the car near Abbot Kinney; you won't need it again until you head home.

The Afternoon

Stop by stop.

1
2:00 PM · Abbot Kinney

Coffee to start

Begin where Venice slows down: a cortado at Intelligentsia, the light-filled roaster that helped set the tone for coffee culture on the Westside. Take it to go and let the caffeine set the pace for the boulevard ahead.

Intelligentsia Coffee · 1331 Abbot Kinney Blvd

2
2:30 PM · The Boulevard

Wander Abbot Kinney

Cup in hand, drift down the commercial spine of Venice — independent boutiques, art galleries, and design studios stacked into low-slung storefronts. Once named "America's coolest street," it's best experienced without an agenda: window-shop, duck into a gallery, and read the neighborhood's creative economy on the storefronts.

Abbot Kinney Blvd, between Venice Blvd & Main St

3
3:15 PM · The Boardwalk

Out to the beach

Cut west toward the water — a ten-minute walk drops you onto the Venice Boardwalk, three miles of oceanfront that doubles as the city's open-air theater. Street performers, artists, and the Pacific on your right the whole way down.

Ocean Front Walk

4
3:45 PM · The Skate Park

Watch the skaters

Pause at the Venice Skatepark, built right into the sand. Its snake run and pool bowls draw some of the best skaters in the country — an easy place to sit on the edge, watch, and understand why this stretch of coast became iconic.

Venice Beach Skatepark · Ocean Front Walk

5
4:15 PM · Muscle Beach

The original outdoor gym

A few steps on is Muscle Beach — the legendary open-air weight pen where bodybuilders have trained in the sun since the 1930s. Watch the workouts, take in the history, and keep moving south along the sand.

Muscle Beach Venice · 1800 Ocean Front Walk

6
5:00 PM · The Canals

Golden hour on the water

Turn inland to the Venice Canals — sixteen blocks of navigable waterways lined with single-family homes, arched footbridges, and gardens spilling toward the water. The public walkways make for the quietest, prettiest stroll in the city, and the light here at golden hour is unmatched.

Venice Canals Historic District

7
7:00 PM · Dinner

The Tasting Kitchen, reopened

End the day back on Abbot Kinney at the newly reopened Tasting Kitchen. Chef Casey Lane's return brings back one of Venice's most beloved dinner rooms — Mediterranean-leaning, ingredient-driven, and worth a reservation. A full-circle close to an afternoon that started a few blocks north with a coffee.

The Tasting Kitchen · 1633 Abbot Kinney Blvd · reservations

A local's tip: the whole loop works on foot from a single parking spot near Abbot Kinney. Go on a First Friday for the boulevard's food trucks and open galleries — or a quiet weekday afternoon if you'd rather have the canals mostly to yourself.

Good to Know

The afternoon, answered.

Where should I get coffee on Abbot Kinney?

Intelligentsia Coffee at 1331 Abbot Kinney Blvd is the neighborhood anchor — a light-filled, design-forward roaster that set the tone for third-wave coffee in Venice. It's the natural first stop before browsing the boulevard's boutiques and galleries.

How far is Abbot Kinney from the beach?

About a 10-to-15-minute walk. Abbot Kinney runs a few blocks inland and parallel to the coast, so you can browse the boulevard, then cut west toward the boardwalk, skate park, and Muscle Beach without moving your car.

Can you walk through the Venice Canals?

Yes. The Venice Canals Historic District has public pedestrian walkways along the water and arched footbridges connecting them. It's free, open to the public, and one of the most peaceful walks in Los Angeles — best in the golden hour before dinner.

Is the Tasting Kitchen open again?

Yes. The Tasting Kitchen, by Chef Casey Lane, has reopened at 1633 Abbot Kinney Blvd. Its return brings back one of Venice's most celebrated dinner rooms — a fitting end to an afternoon that begins and ends on Abbot Kinney.
