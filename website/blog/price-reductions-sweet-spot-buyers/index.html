Nearly 40% of Homes Have Price Reductions — a Sweet Spot for Buyers.

Rising price-reduction rates signal a meaningful shift in negotiating leverage that prepared buyers can take full advantage of right now — if they know how to read the signal.

Michael Abraham
Principal Agent, REALTOR® · Apr 21, 2026 · 5 min read

The real estate market in Los Angeles is undergoing a shift, creating unique opportunities for buyers and sellers alike. With nearly 40% of homes experiencing price reductions, we see an unusual sweet spot in LA's dynamic market that savvy buyers and sellers can leverage.

What's happening in the LA market.

While the national housing market has been cooling, Los Angeles has remained a mixed bag. According to data from the California Association of Realtors (C.A.R.) at the time of writing, LA County has seen a 7% increase in active listings year over year, median prices dipping slightly after years of steady gains, and longer days on market — many properties now sit 45–60 days versus the typical 30. Add elevated mortgage rates, and both sides must adapt quickly to succeed.

For buyers: why now is the time to act.

  • More price reductions = more choices. With cuts more frequent, there's real room to negotiate — you can be selective and target homes previously out of reach.
  • Longer DOM creates leverage. Homes sitting 45+ days often signal motivated sellers willing to accept lower offers to avoid carrying costs or further drops.
  • Affordable pockets are emerging. Mid-City, Echo Park, and the San Fernando Valley are seeing noticeable adjustments — attractive for buyers priced out of the Westside or Downtown.
  • Rates are high, but deals are possible. Price cuts create leverage; a strong negotiated price is permanent, while a rate can be refinanced later.

For sellers: how to regain buyer interest.

  • Price competitively — and strategically. When 40% of listings have adjusted, a token drop may not be enough; a meaningful adjustment creates urgency.
  • Time the season. Buyer activity slows around the holidays; acting decisively ahead of a slowdown captures serious buyers first.
  • Know your submarket. Westside areas like Santa Monica and Brentwood keep strong interest but adjust often; Eastside pockets like Highland Park and Silver Lake are correcting after a long run-up; the South Bay stays steadier.
  • Rescue stale listings. A substantial, well-presented price adjustment can re-energize a listing that's gone quiet — paired with the marketing playbook from our sellers page and the lessons in getting the right offer.

Market data snapshot (at time of writing)

  • Median LA County home price: $831,000 (down 2.8% from the prior quarter).
  • Active listings: 7,100+ (up 7% year over year).
  • Average days on market: 45 (up from 30 a year earlier).
  • 38% of listings had experienced price reductions.

These figures reflect a fundamental shift from the years when homes routinely sold over list within days. Sellers may need to adjust expectations — but buyers can now negotiate with real confidence.

Frequently asked questions.

Does longer time on market mean I can offer less?

Often yes — 45+ days usually signals a motivated seller. It's the first filter we apply when hunting value for buyer clients.

Where are the biggest adjustments happening?

Mid-City, Echo Park, and the San Fernando Valley have seen the most noticeable repricing.

Should sellers panic about price cuts?

No — strategic pricing still wins. Well-priced, well-presented homes continue to sell quickly and competitively.
