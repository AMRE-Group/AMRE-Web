Living in Santa Monica: a neighborhood guide.

Santa Monica is three distinct residential markets, one of the best dining cities on the West Coast, and three and a half miles of Pacific shoreline. Here is what buying and living here actually looks like in 2026.

Michael Abraham
Principal Agent, REALTOR® · May 7, 2026 · 7 min read

Santa Monica is the most expensive city-within-a-city on the Westside of Los Angeles, and for buyers who understand why, the premium is not hard to justify. It sits at the terminus of the 10 freeway and the Metro E Line. It has 3.5 miles of Pacific beachfront. It has an independent school district that outperforms the surrounding system. And it has a level of genuine, everyday walkability that almost no other part of Los Angeles can offer.

Is Santa Monica a good place to live?

By most measures, yes — it is one of the best urban living environments in Southern California. But Santa Monica is not one market. It is three, stacked on top of each other geographically, and they price accordingly.

North of Montana.

The streets north of Montana Avenue, running up to San Vicente Boulevard and toward Santa Monica Canyon, are among the most consistently desirable single-family blocks in Southern California. Entry starts at approximately $3.5–5 million for a well-maintained but unremarkable home on a standard lot, moving into the $6–12 million range for larger properties and meaningful renovations.

The buyer profile skews toward families with school-age children — the Santa Monica–Malibu Unified School District serves this area, and its elementary schools significantly reduce private-school dependency. For buyers in this demographic, the school premium is real and measurable.

Mid-City Santa Monica and the Wilshire corridor.

The mid-city section — south of Montana, north of Pico, between Lincoln and Ocean — is the most varied of the three sub-markets. It includes condominiums along the Wilshire high-rise corridor, single-family homes in established pockets, and the mixed-use activity around Third Street and Main Street. Prices run from $800,000 for a well-positioned condominium to $3 million for a larger single-family home on the best blocks. The Bergamot Station Arts Center — a converted 1875 rail depot with multiple galleries — anchors genuine cultural density within walking distance of residential streets.

Ocean Park and Sunset Park.

South of Pico, Ocean Park and Sunset Park have a different character — more residential, more neighborhood-scaled, more affordable relative to the rest of the city. Single-family homes run approximately $1.4–2.8 million. Proximity to Venice adds a cultural overlay, and beach access is equivalent to any other part of the city. For buyers who want SMMUSD schools and Santa Monica beach access at a lower entry point, this is the sub-market to focus on — we wrote a full guide to Sunset Park, Santa Monica's hidden gem.

Santa Monica is three markets wearing one name — the right sub-market matters more than the right house.

The dining scene.

Santa Monica's restaurant scene is exceptional by any standard. Mélisse — Josiah Citrin's Michelin-starred dining room on Wilshire — has defined LA fine dining for over twenty years. Cassia on Broadway has held a place on every serious best-of list since it opened. Bay Cities Italian Deli on Lincoln has a near-mythological reputation — the Godmother sandwich generates lines that have not abated in decades. Rustic Canyon is the neighborhood's most beloved casual fine dining room, and Gjusta in adjacent Venice is worth any wait at any hour.

The beach, the pier, and the public realm.

Palisades Park — 26 acres of clifftop gardens along Ocean Avenue — offers one of the most dramatically beautiful public spaces in any American city, with unobstructed Pacific views from Malibu to Palos Verdes. Tongva Park, designed by the team behind New York's High Line, is award-winning landscape architecture open to everyone. The Santa Monica Pier, historic since 1909 and the western terminus of Route 66, is one of California's most recognizable landmarks — and for residents, simply a place to walk on a Sunday morning. The Marvin Braude Bike Trail — The Strand — runs 22 miles of coastal path from Pacific Palisades to Palos Verdes; for buyers who cycle, it is commuting infrastructure, not just recreation.

What buyers need to know.

Santa Monica has strict rent control, strong tenant protections, and significant restrictions on short-term rentals. For anyone considering income property, understanding the regulatory environment is essential before committing. The city's planning process is also notably active, so entitlement timelines should be modeled conservatively.

The off-market is meaningful in the north-side single-family market. Homes north of Montana rarely need to advertise — when a property becomes available, the pool of motivated buyers is deep enough that brokers can match a transaction without a public listing. Frequently asked questions.

Is Santa Monica a good place to live?

Widely considered one of the best urban living environments in Southern California — 3.5 miles of beachfront, excellent SMMUSD schools, award-winning public spaces, world-class dining, and Metro E Line rail access.

What is the average home price in Santa Monica?

It varies by sub-market: North of Montana starts at $3.5–5M (up to $6–12M), mid-city condos start around $800K, and Ocean Park / Sunset Park homes run $1.4–2.8M. Santa Monica is consistently among the five most expensive markets in LA County.

What are the best neighborhoods in Santa Monica?

Three primary sub-markets: North of Montana (most prestigious), Mid-City (Wilshire corridor and established blocks), and Ocean Park / Sunset Park (south of Pico, more attainable, close to Venice). All three offer SMMUSD access.

What is North of Montana?

The residential area north of Montana Avenue, generally between 7th Street and the canyon — large lots, mature trees, the Montana Avenue retail corridor, and entry pricing from roughly $3.5–5 million.

Does Santa Monica have rent control?

Yes. The 1979 Rent Control Charter Amendment applies to most rental housing built before 1979, with strict limits on increases and significant eviction protections — investors must model this before purchasing.

What is the Santa Monica school district?

Santa Monica-Malibu Unified (SMMUSD) — consistently one of California's highest-performing public districts, including Santa Monica High (Samohi) and Lincoln Middle School.

How far is Santa Monica from Downtown LA?

About 15 miles by freeway (25–45 minutes by car), or roughly 45–50 minutes on the Metro E Line from 4th/Colorado to 7th/Metro with no transfers.
