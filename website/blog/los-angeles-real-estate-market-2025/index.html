Los Angeles Real Estate Market 2025: Trends & Forecast.

Supply, demand, pricing, and the rental picture across Los Angeles County in 2025 — what the forecasts said, and how the year actually played out.

2025 brought dynamic shifts to Los Angeles real estate — rising sales, firmer prices, and a rental market that never loosened its grip. Here's the year in numbers, and what it set up for 2026.

The 2025 forecast.

The California Association of Realtors (C.A.R.) projected a 10.5% statewide increase in existing single-family home sales — roughly 304,400 units — with Los Angeles County expected to post about 7% year-over-year sales growth and a median price near $909,400. Mortgage rates were forecast to average 5.9%, welcome relief from prior years even if still above pre-pandemic norms.

Affordability & the supply pipeline.

The catch: only about 16% of households could afford a median-priced home. Relief was building on the supply side — single-unit building permits rose 13.2% year over year, and more existing owners returned to the market, gradually easing competition for buyers.

Los Angeles rental market 2025 trends.

With ownership out of reach for most households, the rental market stayed strong all year — especially in suburban submarkets. For investors, sustained demand kept duplexes and small multifamily compelling; we broke down corridor-level numbers in our LA apartment market comparison, and the long arc in 35 years of LA housing appreciation.

Key takeaways

  • 2025 was a growth year: more sales, firmer prices, gently easier rates.
  • Affordability (16%) kept rental demand — and investor opportunity — elevated.
  • Permits (+13.2%) signaled real supply relief in the pipeline.
  • The trends set up the more balanced market we're seeing in 2026.

And a little LA color.

The Hollywood sign began life in 1923 as a real estate ad reading "Hollywoodland." A century later, the headlines rhyme: Jim Jannard's Malibu estate traded for $210 million, and some Malibu owners now install personal fire hydrants — safety upgrades that double as property-value plays.
