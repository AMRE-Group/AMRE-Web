The Los Angeles apartment market, by the numbers.

A year-over-year data snapshot — Q2 2023 vs. Q2 2024 — of prices, cap rates, rents, vacancy, and transaction volume across LA's apartment corridors, and the trends most landlords missed.

Michael Abraham
Principal Agent, Architect · March 15, 2026 · 4 min read

The Los Angeles apartment market experienced subtle but meaningful shifts in key financial indicators between the second quarter of 2023 and the second quarter of 2024. Individually, each move looks small; together, they tell a coherent story about where LA multifamily was heading — one that's still shaping how deals are underwritten today. Here's the snapshot, metric by metric.

Prices: a slight cooling.

The median price per square foot fell 3%, from $357 to $348, while the median price per unit slipped 1%, from $275,000 to $273,000. These adjustments point to a mild cooling — driven by added supply and a shift in investor sentiment rather than any demand collapse.

Cap rates: investors demand more.

The median capitalization rate rose 32 basis points, from 4.57% to 4.89%. Rising cap rates signal investors seeking higher returns — a response to higher perceived risk, tighter financing, and repriced income expectations. For buyers, that's the silver lining: the same building throws off more yield per dollar of purchase price. It's the environment where running the numbers on a value-add deal matters most.

Renters: stable demand, softer edges.

Effective rent per unit rose about 1%, from $2,236 to $2,248 — stable demand, no boom. Vacancy ticked up 23 basis points, from 4.9% to 5.2%, consistent with new development delivering into the market. Neither number, on its own, changes an operating strategy; together they argue for realistic rent-growth assumptions in any pro forma.

The tell: transaction volume rose.

Here's the trend most landlords missed: while prices drifted down, the number of transactions increased 6%, from 279 to 295. Investors weren't retreating — they were buying the adjustment, positioning ahead of anticipated Fed rate cuts. That pattern (softer pricing, rising activity) has historically marked the early innings of a market's turn, not its decline.

Key takeaways

  • LA apartment pricing cooled modestly year over year: $/SF down 3%, price per unit down 1%.
  • Cap rates rose to 4.89% — better going-in yield for disciplined buyers.
  • Rents stayed stable (+1%) while vacancy edged up to 5.2% on new supply.
  • Transaction volume rose 6% — activity increased even as prices adjusted.
