Moving from San Francisco to Los Angeles.

Leaving the Bay but not California? It's one of the cleanest moves there is: your taxes don't change at all — only the price of a home and the number of sunny days. Here's the honest 2026 math, and where SF transplants land in LA.

Michael Abraham
Principal Agent, REALTOR® · June 22, 2026 · 7 min read

You love California. You're just tired of paying San Francisco prices for a foggy one-bedroom while the AI boom pushes the median home toward $2 million. Here's the good news for Bay Area folks eyeing Los Angeles: this is one of the cleanest moves you can make — because the thing everyone worries about, taxes, doesn't change at all.

You stay in California. Same state income tax, same Proposition 13, same rules. What changes is the price of a home and the number of sunny days. The real 2026 numbers:

Same dollar, a lot more home.

This is the headline. San Francisco's median home price sits around $1.7 million in 2026 — and that typically buys a condo or a compact row house. Across LA County, the median is closer to $900,000. The same Bay Area budget can put you in a freestanding house with a yard and a garage, often with hundreds of thousands left over.

The honest caveat: LA's prime beach and Westside blocks — Santa Monica, Venice, the canyons — can still rival San Francisco dollar for dollar. But across the city, your money simply buys more square footage and outdoor space.

The rent gap.

Renting first? A median one-bedroom runs about $3,600/month in San Francisco versus roughly $2,550 in LA — about $1,050 a month back in your pocket, or some $12,000 a year. And while SF rents have been climbing, LA's have softened slightly, widening the gap.

The best part: your taxes don't move.

People relocating between states agonize over tax changes. You don't have to. Because both cities are in California, your state income tax (up to 13.3% at the top), your Prop 13 property-tax basis rules, and your capital-gains treatment are identical. Neither city charges a separate city income tax. The only thing the move lowers is your cost of living. (Approximate, as of 2026; not tax advice.)

What changes — and what doesn't

  • Stays the same: CA state income tax (up to 13.3%), Prop 13 property-tax rules, capital-gains treatment, no city income tax in either city.
  • Gets lower in LA: home prices (about half), 1-bed rent (~$1,050/mo less), price per square foot — and cloudy days.

Yes, there's tech in LA.

The biggest myth Bay Area people carry south is that LA isn't a tech town. It is. Culver City, Playa Vista, and Santa Monica — collectively "Silicon Beach" — host major tech and entertainment-tech employers, and plenty of Bay Area companies run LA offices. Add entertainment, aerospace, and media, and the job market is broader than the Bay's mono-industry intensity.

The lifestyle shift, honestly.

You'll trade Karl the Fog for reliable sun, hills and microclimates for sprawl and freeways, and a compact city for a vast one. Some things you'll miss — the walkability, the transit, the density. But you'll gain space, warmth, a real coastline, and a home that doesn't require winning a bidding war over an $1.8M fixer. For a lot of Bay Area transplants, LA feels like California with room to breathe.

Same taxes, half the home price — the SF-to-LA move is about housing and lifestyle, not spreadsheets.

Frequently asked questions.

Is Los Angeles cheaper than San Francisco?

Yes, mainly on housing. As of mid-2026 the SF median runs about $1.7M versus roughly $900K across LA County — close to half. A median one-bedroom rents for about $3,600 in SF versus about $2,550 in LA.

Do my California taxes change if I move from SF to LA?

No. Both cities are in California — identical state income tax (up to 13.3%), Prop 13 rules, and capital-gains treatment, and neither levies a city income tax. Approximate, as of 2026; not tax advice.

What can I buy in LA for the price of an SF home?

The ~$1.7M that buys a median SF condo or row house can buy a freestanding single-family home with a yard in many desirable LA neighborhoods, with money to spare. Prime beach and Westside blocks can still match SF pricing.

Is there tech work in Los Angeles?

Yes — Silicon Beach (Culver City, Playa Vista, Santa Monica) hosts major tech and entertainment-tech employers, and many Bay Area companies keep LA offices, plus entertainment, aerospace, and media.

Where do San Franciscans usually move in LA?

The Westside tech corridor — Culver City and Playa Vista for Silicon Beach, Santa Monica for coastal walkability — and Sherman Oaks or Mid-City for more space and value.
