Los Angeles: King of the Duplex.

Why LA's unique zoning history, density goals, and lot patterns have made it the single best city in America to own a duplex — both as a homeowner and as an investor. And a real 10-year look at one deal on the Crenshaw corridor.

Michael Abraham
Principal Agent, REALTOR® · DRE# 02242095 · Mar 21, 2026 · 4 min read

Los Angeles has an unusually deep supply of two-unit properties — a legacy of the city's zoning history and lot patterns — and long-run appreciation that few markets match. That combination is why the duplex is such a powerful tool here: you can live in one unit, rent the other, and let the tenant's rent carry much of your cost of ownership while the property appreciates. Take a recently renovated duplex at 2953 S Bronson, in an area experiencing fantastic change that has elevated the entire Crenshaw corridor.

2953 S Bronson — a 10-year analysis.

When considering a property like this, it's crucial to understand the cost of getting started and the potential long-term benefit. Here's the initial investment required, how rental income reduces your carrying cost, and your total return if you sell after ten years.

What it takes to get in

The property is listed at $1,550,000, so a 20% down payment is $310,000. With a 30-year fixed loan at 6.5%, the monthly mortgage payment is about $7,832.68 — roughly $939,921.60 over the first decade. Property taxes and insurance add an estimated $1,937.50 per month combined, typical for a property of this value in LA.

With $4,000 in rent each month, your effective payment drops from $7,832.68 to $3,832.68 right away. Over ten years, rental income totals about $563,338.68, and as rent rises ~3% annually your tenant helps pay down a significant share of the mortgage — cutting the net cost of ownership to roughly $376,582.92 over the decade, an effective monthly payment of about $3,138.19.

At a 7% annual appreciation rate, the property is projected to reach about $3,049,084 after ten years. Selling costs at ~5% run roughly $152,454. Combining net sale proceeds with the ~$563,338.68 of rental income — and subtracting your $310,000 down payment and ~$376,582.92 net carrying cost — total return could reach approximately $3,459,968.68, a profit of around $2,773,385.76. As an investor, I favor value-add, but this is a great way to get into the market, offset carrying cost, and capture accumulated appreciation.

Key takeaways

  • LA's zoning and lot history give it unusually deep duplex supply — ideal for house hacking.
  • Live in one unit, rent the other: here, $4,000 rent cut an $7,833 payment to $3,833.
  • Over 10 years, rent covered a large share of the mortgage and the property nearly doubled in value.
  • Modeled 10-year profit ≈ $2.77M — driven by leverage, rental offset, and LA appreciation.
