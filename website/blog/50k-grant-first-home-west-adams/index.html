How a $50K Grant Opens the Door to Your First Home in West Adams.

A deep dive into the down payment assistance quietly opening doors for first-time buyers in West Adams and other emerging Los Angeles neighborhoods. The Ladder Up program pairs a $50,000 grant with just 3% down — here's how it compares to a traditional loan.

Michael Abraham
Principal Agent, REALTOR® · DRE# 02242095 · Feb 17, 2026 · 4 min read

There's good news if you've been eyeing a home in Los Angeles, particularly in the sought-after West Adams neighborhood: the Ladder Up program could make homeownership far more attainable. It offers a $50,000 grant and requires only 3% down, cutting the upfront costs that stop most first-time buyers. Below is a side-by-side comparison of a traditional 20%-down loan versus Ladder Up on a home priced at $879,000 in West Adams.

What you're actually trading off.

Ladder Up isn't free money without tradeoffs — it's a different structure. A smaller down payment means a higher loan amount, so you can still compete in markets like West Adams, but the monthly payment tends to be higher and typically includes private mortgage insurance (PMI). The upside: grant funds beyond your upfront costs can be used to buy down your interest rate, which lowers the monthly payment and softens that gap. A traditional loan avoids PMI because of the larger down payment — but requires that large down payment in the first place.

Who benefits most

  • First-time buyers struggling to save a substantial down payment.
  • Buyers in high-cost areas like Los Angeles, where cash-to-close is the biggest barrier.
  • Families meeting income requirements — the LA County income limit referenced is $294,600, so many qualify.
  • Buyers who need a co-signer — non-occupying co-borrowers are allowed.

Frequently Asked Questions.

What is the Ladder Up program?

A down payment assistance program for first-time buyers in high-cost areas like LA. It pairs a $50,000 grant with a 3% down requirement, cutting the cash needed to close — and any grant beyond upfront costs can buy down your interest rate.

How much is the grant?

$50,000. On an example $879,000 West Adams home, it fully covers the ~$43,950 needed upfront, taking out-of-pocket cost to close to roughly $0 — versus about $193,380 on a traditional 20%-down loan.

Do I have to be a first-time buyer?

The program targets first-time buyers, especially those who struggle to save a large down payment, and buyers in high-cost markets. It also allows non-occupying co-borrowers if you need a co-signer.

What's the income limit in Los Angeles?

The referenced LA County income limit is $294,600, so many families qualify. Limits change, so confirm current eligibility with a participating lender.

How much do I need to put down?

Just 3%, versus 20% traditionally. The higher loan amount can mean a higher payment with PMI, but grant funds can buy down the rate. You'll also need about two months of reserve payments on hand.
