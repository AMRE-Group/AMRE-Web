Missing the market's secret comeback?

While headlines scream about a sluggish housing market, contract-pending volumes are running 14% higher than this time last year. The data is moving — but most outlets won't catch up for another quarter.

Michael Abraham
Principal Agent, Architect · Mar 30, 2026 · 3 min read

The boldest market analysis is one that's contrarian and bullish. Right now, while coverage of real estate stays gloomy, the real story is different — and it isn't getting the spotlight yet. Home sales are 14% higher in the contract-pending stage than at this same time last year, which means a wave of closings is already locked in. Traditional data lags, so most news outlets can't report it for another quarter.

How far headlines trail the real market

Why the headlines are behind.

Published housing data measures closed sales — the end of a pipeline, not the beginning. By the time a closing appears in a report, the buyer and seller agreed on price months earlier. Pending contracts are the leading indicator, and right now they're pointing up.

What it means for buyers.

A thinner market: with inventory roughly 20% below last year, quality homes attract competition fast — acting quickly and strategically is critical. Price stability: steady prices despite high rates signal that waiting for a significant dip may not pay. The early-mover edge: buying before pending-sales trends hit the news means competing against fewer offers. Our buyer advisory tracks these leading indicators neighborhood by neighborhood.

What it means for sellers.

A pricing window: rising contract signings and steady price signals reward sellers who list ahead of broad recognition. Less competition, more demand: with inventory past its peak, serious buyers have fewer options. The pre-holiday boost: November and December are historically quiet, but this year's pending surge means motivated buyers are closing through year-end.
