A South LA Duplex That Could Build $600K+ in Equity.

How one Hyde Park-area duplex could build you $600,000+ in equity — while you live there. Real numbers on a property where the tenant helps cover your mortgage.

Imagine buying a property where your tenant helps cover the mortgage, rents rise year after year, the home steadily appreciates, and you quietly build hundreds of thousands in wealth — all while having a place to live. That's the power of a wise duplex investment in a neighborhood like Hyde Park, Los Angeles, and it's exactly what a property like 4423 3rd Ave can deliver.

The big picture: what this strategy could mean.

  • Money needed to buy: ~$150,000 down payment + closing costs (80% financing on a $735,000 price)
  • Starting rent: ~$1,800/month for one unit — the tenant helps cover the mortgage
  • Rent over 10 years: ~$235,000+ collected (assuming 3% annual increases)
  • Appreciation: at 6% annual growth, value could climb to ~$1.31M in 10 years — a gain of about $575,000
  • Mortgage paydown: $80,000+ in principal over 10 years

How it works, step by step.

01

Buy the duplex

Owner-occupant financing on a 2–4 unit keeps the down payment and rate far better than an investor loan.

02

Live & rent

You occupy one unit; the tenant's $1,800/mo reduces your out-of-pocket cost from day one.

03

Build & repeat

Fixed mortgage, rising rents, compounding appreciation. Refinance, add an ADU, or leverage equity into the next property.

When you buy a duplex like 4423 3rd Ave, you're not just buying a place to live; you're putting a proven wealth-building engine to work. Your fixed-rate mortgage stays steady while rents rise around you. Each payment chips away at your loan, growing your ownership stake. And as the neighborhood continues to improve, so does the value of your investment. (For the full underwriting framework, see running the numbers on a value-add deal.)

Why this works.

  1. Your tenant helps pay your mortgage every month.
  2. Rents rise over time, improving your financial position.
  3. The property appreciates while your loan payment stays fixed.
  4. You build equity through loan paydown and market growth.
  5. You have options: refinance, add an ADU, or leverage equity into your next property.
What starts as a place to live could become the foundation of over $600,000 in equity in just 10 years.

Key takeaways

  • ~$150K down puts you in a $735K Hyde Park duplex with a tenant offsetting the payment.
  • 10-year picture: ~$575K appreciation + $80K+ paydown + ~$235K rent collected.
  • Owner-occupant financing on 2–4 units beats investor-loan terms.
  • South LA combines accessible pricing with strong rental demand — the core duplex formula.
