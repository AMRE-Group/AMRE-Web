Beverly Hills Post Office — The 90210 Address in LA.

Beverly Hills Post Office carries one of the most famous ZIP codes in the world — 90210 — but it’s actually in Los Angeles, not Beverly Hills. Here’s what BHPO really is, what condos and homes cost in 2026, and what separates it from its famous neighbor below.

Beverly Hills Post Office is one of the most misunderstood addresses in Los Angeles. The name is real, the ZIP code is real — but the neighborhood itself is technically in the City of Los Angeles. Understanding exactly what BHPO is, and what that means for a buyer, is step one of buying there intelligently.

What BHPO Actually Is.

Beverly Hills Post Office — BHPO, or Beverly Crest — is a hillside neighborhood in the City of Los Angeles that sits just north of Beverly Hills city limits. It runs roughly from Mulholland Drive down to a few blocks above Sunset, between Benedict Canyon Drive and Coldwater Canyon. The area has carried the 90210 ZIP code since the Beverly Hills post office began serving deliveries there, but that mailing convenience created a famous quirk: your address reads Beverly Hills, CA 90210 while your city government, police, fire department, and schools are all Los Angeles.

BHPO vs. Beverly Hills — Same ZIP, Different Cities
The takeaway: BHPO offers the 90210 address and the canyon prestige at a lower per-square-foot price than Beverly Hills proper — in exchange for LA city services and LAUSD rather than the independent Beverly Hills city infrastructure.

What It Costs to Buy in BHPO.

BHPO spans a wide price range. The major gated communities — Beverly Park, Mulholland Estates, The Summit, Beverly Ridge Estates — are among the most expensive addresses in the country, with estates trading well above $10 million and the 90210-wide average sale price running around $6–7 million in recent months. But BHPO is not monolithic. Lower BHPO, closer to Sunset, offers entry-level hillside inventory that can trade at or below $1,000 per square foot — meaningfully less than comparable addresses inside Beverly Hills proper.

Condos and Townhomes in BHPO.

BHPO is overwhelmingly a single-family, estate-and-gated-community market. Condos and townhomes exist but are rare — inventory data shows only one or two at any given time across the broader BHPO area. When they do appear they tend to sit within smaller condo complexes or HOA communities near the lower, more accessible edge of the neighborhood, typically priced well below the estate range. For buyers specifically looking for a condo with a 90210 BHPO address, patience and market timing matter more than in any other segment we track.

The 90210 Address Question

Buyers often ask whether the BHPO address — technically “Beverly Hills, CA 90210” — carries the same weight as a Beverly Hills proper address. The prestige is real: the ZIP code is globally recognized and the canyon setting is genuinely spectacular. But a sophisticated buyer will note that the schools are LAUSD, the municipal services are LA city, and the price points — especially at the entry level — reflect that distinction. For buyers who prioritize Beverly Hills city schools, BHPO is not a substitute for a Beverly Hills proper address, even with the matching ZIP.

What the Gated Communities Actually Offer.

The four major BHPO gated communities each have their own character. Beverly Park is one of the most expensive private communities in the US — guard-gated, sprawling estates on multi-acre lots, home to entertainers and executives. Mulholland Estates sits along the ridge, offering canyon-and-city views in a more moderately scaled gated setting. The Summit delivers panoramic views of the LA basin from a ridge-top community. Beverly Ridge Estates is smaller and quieter, closer to Benedict Canyon. Dues, rules, security levels, and minimum lot sizes vary significantly between communities — each warrants individual diligence.

The Architect-Agent Edge on Hillside Property.

BHPO is canyon and hillside real estate — the category where the difference between an architect-trained eye and a generalist matters most. Hillside construction in Los Angeles involves retaining walls, drainage systems, soils reports, geotechnical conditions, and foundation systems that a standard walkthrough simply won’t surface. A view that sells a property can also mean a foundation that cost a fortune to build — and may require more to maintain. An architect-led agent reads the site conditions alongside the listing, tells you what the hillside requires versus what the property delivers, and spots the signals of deferred maintenance that soft-focus photography won’t show.

For BHPO buyers — whether targeting a rare condo, a townhome, or a hillside estate — that structural fluency is the difference between a spectacular purchase and an expensive education.
