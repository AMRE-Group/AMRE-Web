Beverly Hills Post Office — the 90210 address in LA.

BHPO carries one of the most famous ZIP codes in the world — 90210 — but it's actually in Los Angeles, not Beverly Hills. Here's what BHPO really is, what condos and homes cost in 2026, and what separates it from its famous neighbor below.

Michael Abraham
Principal Agent & Licensed Architect · June 12, 2026 · 6 min read

Beverly Hills Post Office is one of the most misunderstood addresses in Los Angeles. The name is real and the ZIP code is real — but the neighborhood itself is technically in the City of Los Angeles. Understanding exactly what BHPO is, and what that means for a buyer, is step one of buying there intelligently.

What BHPO actually is.

Beverly Hills Post Office — BHPO, or Beverly Crest — is a hillside neighborhood in the City of Los Angeles that sits just north of Beverly Hills city limits. It runs roughly from Mulholland Drive down to a few blocks above Sunset, between Benedict Canyon Drive and Coldwater Canyon. The area has carried the 90210 ZIP code since the Beverly Hills post office began serving deliveries there, creating a famous quirk: your address reads Beverly Hills, CA 90210 while your city government, police, fire department, and schools are all Los Angeles. For the wider luxury context, see our guide to navigating luxury real estate in Beverly Hills and Bel Air.

What it costs to buy in BHPO.

BHPO spans a wide price range. The major gated communities — Beverly Park, Mulholland Estates, The Summit, Beverly Ridge Estates — are among the most expensive addresses in the country, with estates trading well above $10 million and the 90210-wide average sale price running around $6–7 million in recent months. But BHPO is not monolithic. Lower BHPO, closer to Sunset, offers entry-level hillside inventory that can trade at or below $1,000 per square foot — meaningfully less than comparable addresses inside Beverly Hills proper.

Condos and townhomes in BHPO.

BHPO is overwhelmingly a single-family, estate-and-gated-community market. Condos and townhomes exist but are rare — inventory data often shows only one or two at any given time across the broader area. When they do appear, they tend to sit within smaller condo complexes or HOA communities near the lower, more accessible edge of the neighborhood, typically priced well below the estate range. For buyers specifically looking for a condo with a 90210 BHPO address, patience and timing matter more than in any other segment we track.

Buyers often ask whether the BHPO address — technically "Beverly Hills, CA 90210" — carries the same weight as a Beverly Hills proper address. The prestige is real: the ZIP code is globally recognized and the canyon setting is spectacular. But a sophisticated buyer will note that the schools are LAUSD, the municipal services are LA city, and the entry-level price points reflect that distinction. For buyers who prioritize Beverly Hills city schools, BHPO is not a substitute for a Beverly Hills proper address, even with the matching ZIP.

Your mail says Beverly Hills, CA 90210. Your city, your services, and your schools say Los Angeles. Knowing the difference is the whole game in BHPO.

What the gated communities actually offer.

The four major BHPO gated communities each have their own character. Beverly Park is one of the most expensive private communities in the US — guard-gated, sprawling estates on multi-acre lots. Mulholland Estates sits along the ridge, offering canyon-and-city views in a more moderately scaled gated setting. The Summit delivers panoramic views of the LA basin from a ridge-top community. Beverly Ridge Estates is smaller and quieter, closer to Benedict Canyon. Dues, rules, security levels, and minimum lot sizes vary significantly between communities — each warrants individual diligence. Frequently asked questions.

What is Beverly Hills Post Office (BHPO)?

BHPO, also called Beverly Crest, is a hillside neighborhood in the City of Los Angeles that carries a Beverly Hills, CA 90210 mailing address. It sits north of Beverly Hills city limits, roughly between Mulholland Drive and Sunset Boulevard, from Benedict Canyon to Coldwater Canyon. Despite the 90210 ZIP, residents fall under LA city services and LAUSD schools.

Are there condos for sale in BHPO?

Condos and townhomes exist but are rare. The area is dominated by single-family estates and gated communities (Beverly Park, Mulholland Estates, The Summit, Beverly Ridge Estates). Occasionally condos or townhomes in smaller complexes near the lower edge come to market; they're uncommon and sell quickly when priced well.

What is the difference between Beverly Hills and BHPO?

Beverly Hills is an independent city with its own services, schools (Beverly Hills Unified), and government. BHPO is a neighborhood inside the City of Los Angeles that shares the 90210 ZIP code — a Beverly Hills mailing address, but LAUSD schools and LA city services, which shows up in entry-level pricing.

What do homes cost in BHPO?

As of mid-2026, broader 90210 single-family medians run roughly $4M–$7M, with top estates well above $10M. Condos and townhomes, when they appear, price below the single-family range, and Lower BHPO closer to Sunset offers more accessible hillside inventory — sometimes at or below $1,000 per square foot.
