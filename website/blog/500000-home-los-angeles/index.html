Monthly carrying costs.

At 6.5% on a 30-year fixed with 20% down, each owner's estimated monthly picture: mortgage ~$2,522, property taxes ~$520, insurance ~$100, maintenance reserve ~$100, shared costs ~$50 — a total of roughly $3,292/mo (PITI + maintenance). Market rent estimates run ~$3,500/mo for the front 3/2 and ~$3,800/mo for the new-build 3/3, so either owner could rent their home at or above break-even — real flexibility if plans change.

ROI & projections.

Assuming 7% annual appreciation and 2% annual rent growth per unit:

  • 5 years: projected value ~$701,755 · net proceeds ~$290,000 · total ROI ~228%
  • 10 years: projected value ~$985,139 · net proceeds ~$648,000 · total ROI ~508%
  • Key metrics, year 1: cash flow ~$208/mo · cash-on-cash ~2.0% · cap rate ~7.0% · DSCR ~1.22 · GRM ~11.3

$500K in LA: FAQs.

Can you really buy a home in LA for $500,000?

Yes — through structures like TIC. Here, a $998,800 duplex splits to $499,400 per unit, each a private 3-bedroom home at roughly half the LA median price.

What is a TIC purchase?

Tenancy in Common: two or more buyers co-own one property, with a written agreement giving each exclusive use of a specific unit. Each finances their share and can generally sell it independently.

Is TIC financing harder?

It's more specialized — fewer lenders, slightly higher rates, thinner resale liquidity. In exchange, buyers typically get meaningfully more home for the money.

Is owning better than renting and investing the difference?

In this model, the renter-investor ends ~$144,000 behind after 10 years — cumulative rent erodes the portfolio while the owner compounds equity.
