Was 2025 the year to buy in Los Angeles?

Wildfires, a supply shock, 7% rates, and a rising median — 2025 tested every buyer assumption in Los Angeles. Here's what the year actually delivered, and the lessons that carry into 2026.

Michael Abraham
Principal Agent, Architect · Feb 6, 2026 · 4 min read

The Los Angeles real estate landscape in 2025 went through a genuine transformation — driven by the January wildfires, evolving market dynamics, and shifting buyer leverage. With the year now behind us, the data tells a clearer story than the headlines did at the time.

The wildfire supply shock.

In January 2025, the Palisades and Eaton fires collectively destroyed more than 16,000 structures. Beyond the human toll, the market effect was immediate: thousands of displaced households competing for housing in a county that was already supply-starved. Every major pressure in the 2025 market traces back, at least in part, to that event.

Prices, rates, and affordability.

The county median reached $886,400 in January 2025 — a 6.4% increase over the prior year — while the 30-year fixed mortgage held around 7%. Forecasts called for a 6–7% band rather than meaningful relief, which is largely how the year played out. As we noted in our 2025 rate update, the buyers who did best treated rates as a budgeting input, not a timing signal.

2025 rewarded prepared buyers more than patient ones. The dip everyone waited for never came.

Rentals and insurance.

Displacement pushed rental demand — and rents — sharply higher in unaffected neighborhoods. At the same time, homeowners across fire-prone areas faced rising insurance premiums, a cost that now belongs in every LA buyer's affordability math. Both trends outlasted the news cycle and remain factors in 2026.

Key takeaways from 2025

  • The wildfire supply shock tightened an already limited market — competition stayed high all year.
  • Prices rose despite 7% rates; waiting for a broad dip was the losing strategy.
  • Insurance costs became a first-order budgeting item for LA buyers.
  • Preparation and flexibility consistently beat market timing.

Frequently asked questions.

Was 2025 a good year to buy real estate in Los Angeles?

For prepared buyers, yes. Supply was tight and rates were high, but prices kept rising — so those who bought well-located homes in 2025 entered 2026 with equity. Those waiting for a correction didn't get one.

How did the wildfires affect the market?

They removed thousands of homes from supply, lifted rents in unaffected areas, and pushed insurance premiums higher across the county — effects still visible in 2026.

Is 2026 a better year to buy?

Conditions are more balanced — inventory has partially recovered and price growth has cooled. For current numbers, read our mid-year 2026 market update.
