Tip: when buying multifamily properties with under-market rents, increasing rents toward market is the single fastest way to improve cash flow — and it directly raises the building's value at sale.

Mortgage, expenses, and cash flow.

Here's what the building costs to run each year:

  • Monthly mortgage payment: $5,734
  • Annual property taxes: $14,362 (1.25% of purchase price)
  • Insurance: $2,500/year
  • Maintenance: 5% of income

Operating expenses on small multifamily usually absorb about 30% of rental income once you include taxes, insurance, maintenance, and management. Budget high — expenses are what quietly eat a pro forma.

The cash-flow curve on this deal:

  • Year 1: slightly negative (−$4,724) during the rent transition
  • Year 2 onward: positive and growing each year
  • Year 5: $22,899/year
Don't panic if Year 1 runs negative. On a value-add multifamily deal, a small first-year deficit is the price of resetting rents — by Year 2 the building pays you.

Exit at a conservative cap rate.

The plan is to sell in Year 5. Priced at a 5% cap rate on the improved income:

  • Projected sale price: $1,834,140
  • Real estate commission (5%): $91,707
  • Net proceeds after loan payoff and fees: $935,079

The cash-on-cash return at sale is 321%, reflecting both the accumulated rental cash flow and the profit from the sale. Always model selling costs up front — and if you plan to roll the gain into the next building instead of cashing out, a 1031 exchange can defer the tax bill. How we underwrite a multifamily deal.

01

Buy the rent gap

Target buildings with rents under market in an appreciating neighborhood — here, $5,559/mo actual vs. $9,700/mo market.

02

Stress the expenses

Model taxes (1.25%), insurance, maintenance at 5% of income, and a full debt load at today's 7% rate — no optimistic shortcuts.

03

Price a conservative exit

Value the Year-5 sale at a 5% cap rate. If the deal still returns 221% ROI on cautious assumptions, it's a real deal.

Tips for first-time multifamily investors.

Down payment & loan. The 25% down payment is $287,250, reducing the loan to $861,750. A larger down payment means smaller mortgage payments and easier cash flow — but don't drain your reserves; keep a buffer for unexpected expenses.

Operating expenses. Expect property taxes ($14,362/year here), insurance ($2,500/year), maintenance (5% of income), and management fees. Estimate high to avoid surprises, and always include a repair buffer.

Cash flow management. Year 1 may run slightly negative (−$4,724 here) while rents reset. By Year 2 the cash flow turns positive, reaching $22,899/year by Year 5. Stay disciplined on the rent schedule.

Exit strategy. Plan for selling costs like the 5% commission ($91,707) from day one, and watch market trends so you sell into strength. Multifamily investing FAQ.

What is a good cap rate for LA multifamily?

Small multifamily in Los Angeles typically trades around a 4.5–5.5% cap rate depending on neighborhood, condition, and how close rents are to market. This analysis deliberately uses a conservative 5% cap for the Year-5 exit — if the market prices the building at a richer cap when you sell, the projected $1,834,140 sale price only improves.

How much down payment do you need for a 4-unit?

Investors typically put 25% down — here, $287,250 on a $1,149,000 purchase, leaving an $861,750 loan. Owner-occupants can often put down less with residential financing. Total cash to close in this deal, including closing costs, is $310,230.

Does a fourplex qualify for residential financing?

Yes. Buildings with 1–4 units qualify for residential mortgages, not commercial loans — one of the fourplex's biggest advantages. This analysis models a standard 30-year fixed at 7%; commercial terms and balloon payments only start at 5+ units.

Is West Adams good for multifamily investing?

West Adams is one of LA's fastest-emerging neighborhoods, and many of its older 2–4 unit buildings still carry under-market rents — the exact setup here, where rents move from $5,559 to a projected $9,700/month. See our full West Adams neighborhood guide.
