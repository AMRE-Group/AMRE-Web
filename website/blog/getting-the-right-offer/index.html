It's Not About Getting Offers — It's About Getting the Right One.

Why the highest offer is rarely the strongest, and how to evaluate competing bids the way an experienced agent does — looking past the headline number to the terms that actually close.

Michael Abraham
Principal Agent, REALTOR® · Mar 6, 2026 · 4 min read

We recently sold 14423 Mulholland Drive, a charming Mid-Century home perched above the valley with panoramic views of Los Angeles, for $1.721 million — 1.5% over asking — in just 8 days. On the surface it looks like a quick, easy win. In reality, this sale is a case study in what it takes to sell a home in today's shifting market: strategy, preparation, negotiation, and patience.

A market that demands more than a listing.

Let's be honest: rates are higher, buyer sentiment is cautious, and inventory is tight in many areas. Homes aren't flying off the shelves like they did during the ultra-competitive frenzy of 2021–2022. Buyers are more intentional, more selective, and slower to decide — especially at higher price points. Simply putting a home on the MLS isn't enough. Success today requires understanding buyer psychology, crafting a compelling narrative, and executing a tailored strategy — the approach behind our full seller playbook:

  • Pricing that attracts attention while leaving no money on the table
  • Preparation so the home photographs and shows beautifully
  • Phased marketing — like a film launch: teaser, trailer, full release
  • Negotiation with clarity, patience, and professionalism

An out-of-state seller, handled end to end.

The Mulholland seller lived entirely out of state. We managed every detail — renovations, vendor coordination, staging, video, photos, and open houses — all funded through Compass Concierge with no upfront cost to the client. Then we executed our 3-Phase Compass Marketing Plan, targeting public and private networks to create buzz and drive qualified traffic.

Negotiation: where the real work happens.

Here's the part most people don't see: once the offers come in, the real work begins. We're not merely discussing numbers; we're navigating personalities, communication, and collaboration with the buyer's agent — finding common ground while keeping the client's best interest at the forefront.

Deals fall apart when egos get in the way. We keep the energy calm and always look for solutions, not just victories.

What sellers need to know right now

  • Buyers are more cautious — your home needs to stand out in both value and story.
  • Price matters, but so does presentation.
  • Marketing is no longer optional — it's strategic and layered.
  • Your listing agent should be project manager, advisor, and negotiator in one.

Whether you're selling in Brentwood, Studio City, Hancock Park, or the Hollywood Hills, the same rule applies: in this real estate market you need more than a sign in the yard. And if you're on the buying side of a negotiation, the mirror-image logic applies — see why price reductions have become a buyer's sweet spot.

Frequently asked questions.

Should I always take the highest offer?

No — the strongest offer balances price with financing strength, contingencies, and certainty of closing. A cleaner, slightly lower offer often nets more with less risk.

How fast can a well-marketed LA home sell?

Mulholland Drive went 1.5% over asking in 8 days. That speed came from preparation and phased marketing, not luck.
