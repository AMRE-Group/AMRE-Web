Moving to Los Angeles — an honest relocation guide.

LA isn't one city — it's dozens of small ones stitched together by freeways. Here's what it really costs to live here in 2026, how to pick the neighborhood that fits your life, and a month-by-month plan to land well.

Michael Abraham
Principal Agent, Architect · Updated July 12, 2026 · 8 min read

Almost everyone who moves to Los Angeles arrives with a picture in their head — palm trees, an ocean breeze, a life that feels a little more open. The good news: that picture is real. The honest part: LA will hand it to you on its own terms, and the people who land well are the ones who understand the city before they sign anything.

Because here's the thing most relocation guides miss: Los Angeles isn't one market — it's dozens of small cities wearing one name. The version of LA you'll love (and afford) depends entirely on which of those small cities you choose. This guide gives you the real numbers, the lifestyle map, and a plan.

What it actually costs.

There's no soft way to say it: LA is expensive. Overall cost of living runs about 50% above the national average, and housing does most of the lifting. As of mid-2026, the median home sale price in the city is roughly $1.0 million — though that single number hides enormous range, from sub-$700K condos in the urban core to multi-million-dollar homes in the hills.

But context matters. If you're coming from the Bay Area — and a lot of people are; San Francisco is currently the metro whose home-searchers look at moving to LA more than any other — these numbers can feel like relief, not sticker shock. Pick your LA, not just a house.

This is the single most important move you'll make. Choose the right neighborhood and almost everything else gets easier — commute, budget, weekends, the whole feel of your day. Choose wrong and even a beautiful house can feel like the wrong life. Start from how you want to live, then work back to the map:

  • Beach & walkable — Santa Monica, Venice, Playa del Rey (~$1.4M+ entry). Read the Santa Monica and Playa del Rey guides.
  • Views & privacy — Bel Air, Hollywood Hills (~$2.0M+). Read the Bel Air guide.
  • Family & space — Sherman Oaks, Studio City in the Valley (~$1.3M+). Read the Sherman Oaks guide.
  • Culture & value — Mid-City, West Adams (~$1.0M+). Read the Mid-City guide.
  • Urban & nightlife — DTLA, Koreatown, Silver Lake (condos from ~$700K).

The most common transplant mistake: signing a 12-month lease in the first neighborhood you tour, sight unseen, before you understand the city. Traffic, vibe, and your actual commute can make two neighborhoods that look identical online feel worlds apart. Give yourself room to learn LA before you lock in.

Anchor on lifestyle first, price second. The buyers who win in LA aren't the fastest — they're the most prepared.

Rent first, or buy now?

If you don't yet know the city, renting for six to twelve months is a smart, low-risk way to test-drive neighborhoods before you commit hundreds of thousands of dollars. If you already know exactly where you want to be and plan to stay several years, buying sooner can make sense — LA home prices have appreciated about 5.3% a year over the last 35 years despite two major downturns (see the 35-year appreciation data). Your move, month by month.

A good relocation isn't a scramble — it's a sequence. Here's the rhythm we walk clients through, whether they're a year out or moving next month.

01

12–6 months out: explore, then visit

Study neighborhoods by lifestyle and budget, then spend weekends in your two or three finalists. The map lies; your commute doesn't.

02

3 months out: get pre-approved

Know your real number before you tour. Pre-approval turns you from a browser into a buyer sellers take seriously.

03

1 month out: tour, offer, land

Work with a local buyer's agent, tour with your short list, and lease short or buy — on your terms, not the market's.

How much you need to earn.

At a roughly $1.0 million median price and 2026 mortgage rates, comfortably buying in the city of LA generally calls for a household income in the low-to-mid six figures plus a meaningful down payment. The good news: widening your search to LA County, or to condos and townhomes, can lower that bar substantially. A local buyer's agent can map your real number to the neighborhoods where it actually goes furthest — our start page is the two-minute way to begin.

Moving to LA at a glance — mid-2026

  • Median home sale price ~$1.0M in the city; condos from under $700K; cost of living ~50% above U.S. average.
  • Pick the neighborhood before the house — lifestyle first, price second.
  • Rent 6–12 months if you don't know the city; buy sooner if you do and plan to stay.
  • LA appreciation has averaged ~5.3%/year over 35 years despite two downturns.

However you arrive — from the Bay, the East Coast, or abroad — the pattern of a good landing is the same: real numbers, the right sub-market, and a sequence instead of a scramble.
