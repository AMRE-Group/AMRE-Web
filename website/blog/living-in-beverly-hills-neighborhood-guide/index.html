Living in Beverly Hills — three markets, one famous ZIP code.

There's the Beverly Hills of television — gates, Rodeo Drive, infinity pools — and the Beverly Hills people actually live in. They overlap, but they aren't the same place. It's an independent city with three distinct home markets, top-ranked schools, and a dining scene that rivals any in the country. Here's the honest read for buyers.

Michael Abraham
Principal Agent, Architect · May 7, 2026 · 7 min read

Beverly Hills is not a neighborhood of Los Angeles. It is an independent city, fully encircled by the City of LA, with its own police department, its own fire department, and its own school district. That distinction carries real consequences — for property taxes, for services, and most of all for education. Understanding it is the first step to buying here well.

Three markets, one ZIP code.

The single most important thing to understand about Beverly Hills real estate is that one address covers fundamentally different property types at fundamentally different prices — and each market behaves differently from the others.

01

The Flats

Level streets between Sunset and Wilshire — Maple, Elm, Camden, Bedford, Linden. Among the most consistently valuable urban land in the country. Entry runs ~$4.5–6M; renovations and larger lots reach $8–15M.

02

Trousdale Estates

Carved into the hills in the 1950s — homes by Lloyd Wright, Buff & Hensman, Hal Levitt, and Paul Williams on ridgeline lots. You're buying design history; architecture-literate due diligence is not optional here.

03

Beverly Hills Post Office (BHPO)

A Beverly Hills mailing address that sits inside LA city limits — meaning LA property taxes, LA schools, and LA permitting. Know exactly what a BHPO address does and doesn't include.

The city is fully built out. Every meaningful transaction is a transfer of existing estate fabric — which creates a permanent floor that demand-side pressure cannot dissolve.

The school-district advantage.

Beverly Hills Unified is one of the highest-performing public school districts in California, and it is entirely self-contained within the city's boundaries. For families, a home in the Flats delivers walking-distance access to top-ranked public schools without the private-school overhead most of Los Angeles requires. That premium is materially real — it shows up in price, in demand, and in days on market. It's also the clearest reason the Flats and BHPO markets, despite sharing an address, don't trade alike.

Where to eat, and what to do.

The dining here is exceptional. Spago on North Cañon has defined California cuisine for forty years; the Polo Lounge at the Beverly Hills Hotel has hosted power breakfasts since 1941; Jean-Georges at the Waldorf Astoria brings modern French to the Wilshire corridor; and Nate'n Al on Beverly Drive remains a cherished institution. Culturally the city punches well above its size — the Academy Museum of Motion Pictures in its Renzo Piano building, Greystone Mansion's 18-acre grounds, and the Wallis Annenberg Center anchor the scene. For the outdoors, Franklin Canyon Park offers 605 acres of urban wilderness minutes from the Flats, and Beverly Gardens Park runs 1.9 walkable miles along Santa Monica Boulevard.

Key takeaways for buyers

  • It's a city, not a neighborhood — own police, fire, and BHUSD schools, separate from LA.
  • Three sub-markets: the Flats (~$4.5–6M entry), Trousdale ($8–25M+), and BHPO.
  • BHPO ≠ Beverly Hills proper: LA taxes, LA schools, LA permitting — despite the address.
  • Off-market matters: 30–40% of $5M+ properties transact privately; agent relationships are the way in.
  • Scarcity is structural: the city is built out, so values have held through every cycle.

What the market looks like right now.

Beverly Hills has held its value through every cycle because its scarcity is structural — no new land, no development pipeline. Off-market activity is significant: at $5 million and above, a meaningful share of the best Flats and hills properties never touch the MLS. Sellers at this level prefer discretion, and buyers who aren't connected to the right agent simply never see the best inventory. FAQ

Living in Beverly Hills, answered.

Is Beverly Hills a city or a neighborhood?

Beverly Hills is an independent city, not a neighborhood of Los Angeles. It is fully encircled by the City of LA but has its own police, fire, and school district (BHUSD). That independence has real consequences for schools, services, and property. One catch: some "Beverly Hills Post Office" (BHPO) addresses carry a Beverly Hills ZIP but sit inside LA city limits.

Is Beverly Hills a good place to live?

For buyers who value top-ranked public schools, walkable luxury, and structurally stable home values, yes. Beverly Hills Unified is one of California's highest-performing districts, the Flats are among the most consistently valuable urban land in the country, and the dining and culture rival any city. The trade-off is cost: entry in the Flats runs about $4.5–6M in 2026.

What are the neighborhoods in Beverly Hills?

Three sub-markets dominate. The Flats — the level streets between Sunset and Wilshire (Maple, Elm, Camden, Bedford, Linden) — are prime walkable land. Trousdale Estates, carved into the hills in the 1950s, holds much of California's most significant mid-century architecture. Beverly Hills Post Office (BHPO) uses a Beverly Hills address but lies within LA city limits.

How much does it cost to live in Beverly Hills?

Entry-level homes in the Flats start around $4.5–6 million in 2026, with renovated and larger parcels reaching $8–15M. Trousdale Estates ranges from roughly $8M to $25M and above. The city is fully built out, so scarcity is structural and values have held through every cycle.

What is the difference between Beverly Hills and BHPO?

BHPO — Beverly Hills Post Office — properties carry a Beverly Hills mailing address but sit within Los Angeles city limits. That means LA property taxes, LA schools (not BHUSD), and LA permitting. Buyers drawn primarily by the address should understand what a BHPO purchase does and doesn't include before committing.
