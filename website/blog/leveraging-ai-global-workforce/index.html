The local quote that started the experiment

From local to global: my architect story.

On a recent ADU project, a talented local architect quoted over $30K. The quality was there; the cost wasn't justifiable for early-stage work. On Upwork I found an equally skilled architect in Moldova at nearly 1/20th of the price for concept design and drafting. I'll still bring in a local architect to finalize plans and handle city regulations — but blending global talent for early phases with local expertise for compliance kept the project moving without overspending.

The Buy Back Your Time principle.

Dan Martell's Buy Back Your Time makes one essential point: you don't need more hours in the day — you need to reclaim them by delegating what others can do better or cheaper. By outsourcing admin and specialized tasks, and letting AI handle repetitive workflows like email management and data entry, I spend my time where it compounds: client relationships and finding the best deals. It's the same philosophy behind how we think about AI vs. human judgment and where the industry is heading.

The goal isn't just to free up time — it's to use that time to scale your business and your life.
01

Audit your hours

Identify repetitive, low-impact tasks — admin, scheduling, research, drafting.

02

Delegate & automate

Source global talent (Upwork, Fiverr) and let AI absorb the repetitive workflows.

03

Reinvest the time

Client relationships, deal-finding, strategy — the work only you can do.

Making it work in practice.

The operating checklist we actually use: identify what can be done remotely; get local quotes first as a benchmark; break projects into phases and outsource the early ones; keep local experts for permits and compliance; manage everything with clear deliverables through tools like Slack, Zoom, and Asana; and review the system regularly. The same discipline applies whether you're running a team or underwriting a value-add investment.

Key takeaways

  • Global talent can cut early-phase project costs by an order of magnitude.
  • Keep local experts for permits, compliance, and final sign-off.
  • Let AI absorb repetitive workflows; reinvest hours in relationships and deals.
  • Audit your time regularly — delegation is a system, not a one-off.

Blending AI, a global workforce, and strategic outsourcing is how you work smarter in Los Angeles real estate — and how we keep AMRE focused on the parts of the business that matter most to clients.
