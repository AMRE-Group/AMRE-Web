Palm Springs Heats Up This Summer: Things to Do + the Market.

Pool parties, summer-rate golf, pickleball, arena shows, and a dining scene that doesn't take summers off — plus why the desert's $700K–$1M sweet spot makes summer the smart season to buy.

Michael Abraham
Principal Agent, Architect · Jun 26, 2026 · 7 min read

There's a long-running joke that Palm Springs in July is where you go to feel your sunglasses melt. But the secret the snowbirds never learn is that summer is when the desert is most fun, least crowded, and most generous with a deal — in the restaurants, on the tee sheet, and in the housing market.

The dining scene doesn't take summers off.

Summer is when the desert's kitchens exhale — tables open up and summer menus appear. Lead with Bougainvillea Fresh Cuisine, the late-2025 downtown arrival from chef-owner Alex Hernandez; pour a glass at Vinophile Café, then climb to the moody After Hours lounge above Sunset. Caffeinate at Coffeeism Co., go kitschy at Bootlegger Tiki, and book a shaded patio at the perennial favorite Farm. Summer is the only season you can walk into the desert's hottest new restaurant — in both senses of the word.

Pool parties & performances after dark.

Splash House returns August 7–9 and 14–16, taking over Palm Springs hotels with day parties and headline DJs. Twenty minutes down-valley, Acrisure Arena keeps the marquee lit: Lionel Richie with Earth, Wind & Fire (Aug 8), Ramón Ayala (Aug 29), and Chicago & Styx (Sept 2).

Mark the calendar

  • Splash House — Aug 7–9 & Aug 14–16, across Palm Springs hotels (21+).
  • Lionel Richie + Earth, Wind & Fire — Acrisure Arena, Aug 8.
  • Ramón Ayala — Acrisure Arena, Aug 29.
  • Chicago & Styx — Acrisure Arena, Sept 2.

Golf at summer prices, pickleball everywhere.

The courses you can't book in February go wide open — and dramatically cheaper — in July: Pete Dye's Stadium Course at PGA West, old-school Indian Canyons where Sinatra's photos still hang, simulator golf at The Golf Bar and Topgolf Swing Suite, and a fully lit 18 under the stars at The Lights at Indio. Meanwhile the Coachella Valley has become one of the most pickleball-obsessed corners of the country — courts at nearly every resort, timed neatly around the heat.

Beating the heat, desert-style.

The Aerial Tramway rotates you up nearly 6,000 feet to pine forest that runs ~30°F cooler. The Palm Springs Surf Club makes the heat the point; The Spa at Séc-he soaks it away over a natural mineral hot spring; and Indian Canyons and Tahquitz Canyon are cinematic at sunrise.

The market: summer is the buyer's season.

Here's the part we watch professionally: the desert's $700K–$1M single-family range is the sweet spot, and summer means more choice and less competition. Sellers who list in July are motivated; buyers who shop in July have leverage that vanishes when the snowbirds return.
