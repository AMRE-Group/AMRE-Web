Palm Springs Heats Up This Summer

Yes, it's hot. That's rather the point. Behind every triple-digit afternoon is a pool that opens at dawn, a tee time at half the winter price, and a dinner reservation you can actually get. Here is your guide to a desert summer done right — plus why the season is quietly one of the best times to buy.

There is a long-running joke that Palm Springs in July is where you go to feel your sunglasses melt. Locals will tell you the heat is "air you can lean on." And every one of them will say it from a chaise longue, in the shade, with something cold in hand — because the secret the snowbirds never quite learn is that summer is when the desert is most fun, least crowded, and most generous with a deal.

The pools are not decorative. The mountains stay cool at the top. The new restaurants are taking reservations. And the real estate market? It's having a genuinely good moment for buyers. Let's take it section by section.

01 — Eat & Drink

The Dining Scene Doesn't Take Summers Off

Summer is when the desert's kitchens exhale. The winter crush is gone, the tables open up, and many rooms roll out summer menus and happy hours that make the season the smart time to eat well. It's also the best moment to try the wave of newcomers that have transformed Palm Springs dining.

Lead with Bougainvillea Fresh Cuisine, which opened downtown in late 2025 — chef-owner Alex Hernandez plates refined, Latin-inspired dishes (yes, including a genuinely excellent pasta program) from an intimate open kitchen. Pour a glass at Vinophile Café, the European-style wine bar that's become a downtown fixture, then climb the stairs to After Hours, the moody cocktail lounge above Sunset on North Palm Canyon Drive. Caffeinate at Coffeeism Co., go gloriously kitschy at Bootlegger Tiki, and book a shaded patio at the perennial favourite Farm. The through-line: there is now something genuinely new worth trying in every direction.

Summer is the only season you can walk into the desert's hottest new restaurant — in both senses of the word.
02 — Music & Nightlife

Pool Parties and Performances

After DarkThe desert's nightlife has grown up. Marble-topped cocktail rooms, upstairs lounges and design-forward bars now keep downtown humming long after the pools close — and the summer crowd is the in-the-know one.

If Palm Springs has an official sound, in summer it's a bassline echoing off a hotel pool deck. Splash House — the desert's beloved poolside music festival — returns for two weekends, August 7–9 and August 14–16, taking over Palm Springs hotels with day parties, headline DJs and an after-hours component for the dedicated. It is summer in the desert distilled into a wristband.

For the big stage, Acrisure Arena — about twenty minutes down-valley in Palm Desert — keeps the marquee lit through the heat. The summer slate includes Lionel Richie with Earth, Wind & Fire (August 8), Ramón Ayala (August 29) and Chicago & Styx (September 2), plus touring shows all season. The same arena is home to the AHL's Coachella Valley Firebirds — proof the desert now does indoor, air-conditioned spectacle on a serious scale.

  • Lionel Richie + Earth, Wind & Fire — Acrisure Arena, Aug 8

03 — On the Course

Tee It Up at Summer Prices

Here is the open secret of desert golf: the courses you can't afford or can't book in February become wide open — and dramatically cheaper — in July. Play Pete Dye's famous Stadium Course at PGA West at reduced summer rates, or get a double dose of old-school Palm Springs at Indian Canyons Golf Resort, where the photos of one-time regular Frank Sinatra still hang in the clubhouse.

Prefer your golf air-conditioned? The desert has quietly become a simulator capital. Swing through more than 100 courses on pro-grade tech at The Golf Bar in La Quinta, play Pebble Beach without leaving the AC at the Topgolf Swing Suite in Cathedral City, or dial in your bag at PGA Tour Superstore. And when it finally cools after dark, The Lights at Indio offers a fully lit 18 you can walk under the stars.

In winter, these tee sheets are booked solid. In summer, the course — and the rate — is yours.
04 — Game On

The Pickleball Capital of the Desert

Somewhere along the way, the Coachella Valley became one of the most pickleball-obsessed corners of the country — and summer hasn't slowed it down a bit. Courts have sprouted at resorts, community centres and dedicated clubs across the valley, with early-morning and evening sessions timed neatly around the heat. Many hotels now treat a few courts as standard issue, alongside the pool and the fire pit, which means you can roll out of your room and into a game before breakfast. Pack a paddle (or rent one); it has become the desert's default social sport.

05 — Beat the Heat

Cool Escapes, Hidden in Plain Sight

The trick to a desert summer is knowing where the cool is — and it's closer than you'd think. The Palm Springs Aerial Tramway rotates you up nearly 6,000 feet to Mount San Jacinto State Park, where it can run about 30 degrees cooler than the valley floor — pine forest, hiking trails and a sweater in August. Back at sea level, the Palm Springs Surf Club turns the heat into the whole point, with surf sessions, a lazy river and shaded cabanas. For something restorative, soak at The Spa at Séc-he, the Agua Caliente Band's modern downtown spa built around a genuine natural mineral hot spring. And the hikes through Indian Canyons and Tahquitz Canyon are glorious at sunrise — palm oases, waterfalls and the desert at its most cinematic before the day warms up.

When the thermostat wins, head here
  • Aerial Tramway — up to the pines, ~30°F cooler
  • Palm Springs Surf Club — surf, lazy river, cabanas
  • The Spa at Séc-he — downtown mineral-springs soak
  • Indian Canyons & Tahquitz — sunrise hikes to palm oases
06 — Where to Stay

Check In — Resorts at Their Best Rates

Summer is when the desert's hotels are most attainable, which makes it the ideal season to test-drive the places everyone fights over in winter. The headliner remains Thompson Palm Springs, the 168-room luxury hotel that opened downtown in late 2024 and landed on Condé Nast Traveler's 2026 Hot List. For design lovers, Drift Palm Springs pairs minimalist rooms with modern Baja cooking, while intimate hideaways like La Serena Villas, Casa Cody and Colony Palms define the small-and-stylish end. Stay a weekend, fall for a neighbourhood, and — as a lot of people quietly do — start wondering what it would cost to have a place of your own here. Which brings us to the good news.

07 — The Market

And Now, the Good News for Buyers

If the city is heating up, the buying conditions are refreshingly cool. The story this summer is the single-family home — and specifically the $700,000 to $1 million band, which has quietly become the desert's sweet spot. It's the range where you stop compromising: a detached house, room to breathe, very often a private pool and mountain views, and the kind of mid-century character buyers fly in for — all while sitting comfortably below the $1M-plus luxury tier.

The wider market backdrop makes the case. Palm Springs' median home sale price has hovered around $651,000 this spring, up roughly 1% year over year — steady appreciation, not a frothy run-up. That puts $700K–$1M right at the heart of the move-in-ready detached market: enough to buy genuine quality, without the premium that kicks in north of seven figures. Add a healthy supply of listings and you get something buyers haven't had in years — choice, and time to make a smart decision.

$700K to $1M is where Palm Springs stops asking you to compromise — a pool, the views, the mid-century character, all under the luxury line.

It adds up to a rare alignment for buyers. Prices are appreciating gently rather than spiking, the $700K–$1M range delivers the full Palm Springs experience — pool, patio, mountain backdrop, mid-century bones — and the summer calendar thins out the competition that crowds the winter market. For a second home or a primary move, this is precisely the window when a prepared buyer can act with confidence and without a bidding war. The city keeps getting better every season; the smart play is to be holding the keys before the crowd flies back in for the cool weather.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it too hot to visit Palm Springs in summer?

Summer days run hot — often above 100°F — but the desert is built for it: pools open early, resorts drop to their best rates of the year, and cool escapes like the Aerial Tramway (around 30°F cooler at the top) are minutes away. With pool parties, summer-rate golf and arena shows, many regulars consider summer the most fun — and most affordable — season.

What is there to do in Palm Springs this summer?

Highlights include the Splash House poolside music festival (August 7–9 and 14–16), concerts at Acrisure Arena such as Lionel Richie with Earth, Wind & Fire (August 8) and Chicago & Styx (September 2), summer-rate golf across the valley, a thriving pickleball scene, the Palm Springs Surf Club and the Aerial Tramway. The downtown dining scene stays open and far easier to book than in winter.

Is summer a good time to buy a home in Palm Springs?

For many buyers, yes. The market offers a healthy supply of single-family listings, prices have been appreciating only gently (median sale price around $651,000, up about 1% year over year), and there is typically less buyer competition than the winter high season. As always, the right move depends on your goals — a local advisor can help you read the sub-market.

What does $700,000 to $1 million buy in Palm Springs?

This is the desert's single-family sweet spot. In the $700K–$1M range you'll generally find move-in-ready detached homes — frequently with a private pool, outdoor living space, mountain views and mid-century character — while staying below the $1M-plus luxury tier. With the citywide median sale price around $651,000, this band buys genuine quality without paying the luxury premium.

Where can I golf in Palm Springs in the summer?

Many courses offer reduced summer rates, including Pete Dye's Stadium Course at PGA West and the historic Indian Canyons Golf Resort. For air-conditioned play, try simulator venues like The Golf Bar (La Quinta), the Topgolf Swing Suite (Cathedral City) or PGA Tour Superstore — and The Lights at Indio offers fully lit night golf.
