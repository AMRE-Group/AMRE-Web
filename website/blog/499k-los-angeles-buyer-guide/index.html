What is a TIC?

A tenants-in-common arrangement lets multiple owners each hold a fractional interest in one property, with the exclusive right to occupy a specific unit. Unlike a condo — where each unit is legally subdivided and individually deeded — TIC owners share a single deed and a TIC agreement that spells out who lives where and who pays what. Because TICs skip the slow, expensive condo-conversion process, they typically sell for meaningfully less than a comparable condo. That price gap is the whole point.

TIC vs. condo — the real difference.

The confusion is understandable, but the distinction is simple and it drives everything from price to financing:

  • Deed: A condo is an individually deeded unit. A TIC is a percentage share of one shared deed.
  • Price: TIC units in Los Angeles typically run 20–35% below a comparable condo in the same area.
  • Financing: Condos use standard mortgages; TICs use a fractional loan (more on that below).
  • Governance: Condos have HOA bylaws; TICs run on a private TIC agreement between owners.

How TIC financing works.

This is the question that used to kill TIC deals — and the reason the market has grown. In today's Los Angeles TIC market, most buyers use a fractional loan: an individual mortgage tied only to your share, so you're never on the hook for a co-owner's payments. A handful of LA lenders specialize in it.

Are TICs a good buy?

For buyers priced out of condos and houses, a well-chosen TIC is a legitimate way to stop renting and start building equity in a neighborhood you actually want. The honest trade-offs: shared ownership, a TIC agreement worth reviewing carefully, slightly higher loan rates than a standard mortgage, and a smaller resale pool. With the right building and financing, those trade-offs are very manageable — and that's exactly the diligence we run on every TIC.

Key takeaways

  • TIC units in Los Angeles regularly sell under $500K — well below comparable condos.
  • Fractional TIC loans finance your share only, typically with 10–20% down.
  • A condo is individually deeded; a TIC is a share of one deed plus a TIC agreement.
  • The right building + a solid TIC agreement make it a real path to LA homeownership.
