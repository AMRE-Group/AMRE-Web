Sunset Park, Santa Monica: A Neighborhood & Buyer Guide.

Why this underpriced Santa Monica neighborhood is quietly attracting some of the most discerning buyers on the Westside — and what home prices, schools, and the airport-park conversion mean for value-seekers in 2026.

Michael Abraham
Principal Agent, Architect · Updated July 8, 2026 · 8 min read

Sunset Park is Santa Monica's most family-friendly — and most undervalued — sub-market. Nestled in the southern part of the city, it balances laid-back, tree-lined residential streets with quick access to the beach, Main Street, and the freeways. People search for it as both "Sunset Park Santa Monica" and "Sunset Park Los Angeles," and the short answer is the same: it delivers Santa Monica quality of life at a meaningfully lower price than the Westside's marquee blocks.

Where is Sunset Park?

Sunset Park sits in the southern portion of Santa Monica, bounded by Pico Boulevard to the north, Lincoln Boulevard to the west (where it meets Ocean Park), Marine Street to the south, and the Santa Monica Airport to the east. It falls primarily within zip code 90405. The neighborhood was originally developed in the 1940s to house Douglas Aircraft workers and retains a residential grid of modest single-family homes on 6,000–7,000-square-foot lots — generous by central-LA standards, if modest next to North of Montana's larger parcels.

Home prices & the 2026 market.

The headline most people quote — a median sale price of roughly $2.5 million as of early 2026, up about 43% year-over-year — is real but partly a composition story. Larger, more thoroughly renovated homes have transacted at higher rates, pulling the median up; the fuller 12-month average sits closer to $2.0 million. Median price per square foot is around $1,550, and homes typically sell in 38–41 days, faster than the broader Santa Monica market. Two structural drivers stand out: post-fire displacement demand concentrating in flat-lot, lower-density 90405, and the airport-to-park conversion adding a major open-space amenity next door.

North of Montana, for context, commands roughly double Sunset Park's price — around a $3.85M single-family median — with access to Roosevelt and Franklin, SMMUSD's strongest-ranked elementaries. Sunset Park trades that prestige and the absolute-top schools for meaningfully better square-foot-per-dollar, broader lots, R2 zoning that enables an ADU or guest house, and the very same SMMUSD district.

Buyers who want Santa Monica's quality of life without the $4M North-of-Montana entry point have a standard answer: Sunset Park.

Schools: Will Rogers & Grant.

Sunset Park is part of the Santa Monica-Malibu Unified School District (SMMUSD), and the two elementary assignments matter. Will Rogers Learning Community is one of SMMUSD's most-requested elementaries, with a strong academic reputation and a tight parent community; its boundary covers much of central and eastern Sunset Park. Grant Elementary, also highly rated, covers more of the western side near Lincoln. Because the line between them runs through the middle of the neighborhood, verify the assignment by exact address with SMMUSD before you write an offer. Both feed into John Adams Middle School and Santa Monica High School (Samohi).

The airport-park tailwind.

The Santa Monica Airport, which forms Sunset Park's eastern boundary, is being converted into a large regional park. The conversion has been a meaningful tailwind: 90405 property values have measured up roughly 12% since early 2025, with the airport-park transition cited as a key catalyst, and the east-side blocks abutting Clover Park and the future park land are arguably the strongest appreciation pocket of Sunset Park in 2026. The airport is still operational pending full closure, and timing has shifted more than once — so price the current reality and plan for the long-term upside.

Sunset Park vs Ocean Park.

The two are neighbors — and often confused. Ocean Park sits west of Lincoln, closer to the beach and Main Street, denser and more walkable (Walk Score in the 90s), with price per square foot near $1,875. Sunset Park sits east, with quieter residential streets, a grid layout, and a more family-oriented atmosphere, typically pricing 15–20% lower per square foot while offering more square footage and yard. For families prioritizing space and schools over walkability to the beach, Sunset Park is often the better fit. Pearl (north, entry point), the central 14th–20th streets (family core), Marine (south), and the park-adjacent east all price and trade differently.

03

Run the restoration math

Many homes date from the 1940s. Original-condition listings can carry higher rehab costs — plumbing, electrical, seismic retrofit — than buyers expect.

Living in Sunset Park at a glance

  • Santa Monica's most family-friendly, undervalued sub-market — quieter grid streets, strong SMMUSD schools.
  • ~$2.5M median (12-mo avg closer to $2.0M), ~$1,550/sq ft, homes selling in 38–41 days.
  • Primary zip 90405; Will Rogers & Grant elementary; feeds John Adams MS and Samohi.
  • Airport-to-park conversion is a measured tailwind — ~12% appreciation in 90405 since early 2025.

The buyers who do best here get specific about three things up front: the elementary boundary their target address falls into, the sub-area trade-offs, and the realistic restoration math on Douglas-era homes. Get those right, and Sunset Park delivers Santa Monica living at a lower-than-Westside price.
