Sunset Park: Santa Monica's Hidden Gem.

Why this underpriced Santa Monica neighborhood is quietly attracting some of the most discerning buyers on the Westside — and what that means for value-seekers in 2026.

Michael Abraham
Principal Agent, AMRE Real Estate Group · June 15, 2026 · 8 min read

Nestled in the southern part of Santa Monica, Sunset Park is a neighborhood that balances laid-back living with the convenience of urban amenities. Known for tree-lined streets and a close-knit community, it offers a serene escape while remaining close to everything Santa Monica provides — and it does it at a meaningfully lower price than the Westside markets that surround it.

Key takeaways

  • ZIP 90405, originally built in the 1940s for Douglas Aircraft workers.
  • Median sale price ~$2.5M (early 2026); 12-month average closer to $2.0M.
  • Same SMMUSD district as North of Montana — at roughly half the entry price.
  • The Santa Monica Airport is converting to a regional "Great Park" — a real appreciation catalyst.
  • High concentration of R2-zoned lots makes many parcels ADU-friendly.

A prime location with easy access.

Sunset Park is bordered by Pico Boulevard to the north, Lincoln Boulevard to the west, Venice to the south, and the Santa Monica Airport to the east — providing easy access to the beach and downtown Santa Monica. Its proximity to nearby freeways also makes commuting to the rest of Los Angeles convenient, adding to the appeal for buyers who want both tranquility and accessibility.

An eclectic mix of homes.

Sunset Park's architectural landscape is as diverse as its community. From classic California bungalows and mid-century homes to contemporary builds, the variety adds to the neighborhood's charm. The mix of single-family homes, duplexes, and small apartment buildings serves a wide range of residents, from young professionals to growing families. Clover Park anchors the family-friendly side of the neighborhood with green space, sports fields, and playgrounds; Ocean Park Boulevard lines up the cafés, restaurants, and boutiques.

The Sunset Park real estate market in 2026.

The headline number most people quote about Sunset Park — a median sale price of roughly $2.5 million as of early 2026, up about 43% year-over-year — is real but partly a composition story. Larger, more thoroughly renovated homes have transacted at higher rates, pulling the median up; the fuller 12-month average sits closer to $2.0 million. Median price per square foot is approximately $1,550, and homes typically sell in 38–41 days, faster than the broader Santa Monica market.

The structural drivers of recent price action are unusually clear. First, post-fire displacement demand from buyers seeking flat-lot, lower-density Santa Monica away from the hillside has concentrated heavily in 90405. Second, the Santa Monica Airport conversion to a regional "Great Park" has added a major open-space amenity directly adjacent to the neighborhood — roughly 12% price appreciation in 90405 since early 2025, with the airport-park transition cited as a key catalyst. This is the kind of quiet, structural repricing we flagged in the market's secret comeback.

Schools — the SMMUSD calculus.

Sunset Park is part of Santa Monica-Malibu Unified. Two elementary assignments matter: Will Rogers Learning Community (PreK–5), one of SMMUSD's most-requested elementaries, covering much of central and eastern Sunset Park; and Grant Elementary (K–5), also highly rated, covering more of the western side near Lincoln. The boundary runs through the middle of the neighborhood — verify by exact address with SMMUSD. Both feed John Adams Middle School and Santa Monica High School (Samohi). The comparison most buyers make is Sunset Park vs. North of Montana schools: for families who want the district without the $4M+ entry point, Sunset Park is the standard answer.

A note for ADU-curious buyers

  • Sunset Park has one of the highest concentrations of R2-zoned lots in Santa Monica.
  • Many parcels permit a guest house or accessory dwelling unit (ADU) — useful for a back-house rental or multi-generational living.
  • Always confirm the specific zoning of a prospective lot with the Santa Monica Planning Department before writing an offer.

The honest trade-offs.

  • Airport noise (for now). The airport is still operational pending full conversion; eastern blocks hear it. Price in the current reality, plan for the long-term upside.
  • Compact lots vs. NoMo. 6,000–8,000 sq ft lots are the norm — generous by central LA standards but modest next to North of Montana.
  • Restoration risk on Douglas-era stock. Many homes date to the 1940s; original-condition listings can carry higher rehab costs for plumbing, electrical, and seismic work.
  • Heat without ocean breeze on the eastern edge. Far enough from the beach that marine-layer cooling thins by midday in summer. AC matters, even in Santa Monica.

Frequently asked questions.

Where is Sunset Park in Santa Monica?

Sunset Park sits in the southern portion of Santa Monica, bounded by Pico Boulevard (north), Lincoln Boulevard (west, separating it from Ocean Park), Venice/Marine Street (south), and the Santa Monica Airport (east). It's primarily within ZIP 90405, originally developed in the 1940s for Douglas Aircraft workers.

What is the median home price in Sunset Park in 2026?

The median sale price is approximately $2.5 million (early 2026), up ~43% YoY; the 12-month average sits closer to $2.0M. Median $/sqft is ~$1,550, and homes sell in 38–41 days. Most single-family transactions fall between $1.8M and $3M.

What schools serve Sunset Park?

SMMUSD (Santa Monica-Malibu Unified). Two elementary assignments — Will Rogers Learning Community or Grant Elementary, depending on the exact block — feeding John Adams Middle School and Santa Monica High School (Samohi). Verify by exact address; the boundary runs through the neighborhood.

How is Sunset Park different from Ocean Park?

Adjacent, separated by Lincoln Blvd. Ocean Park sits west, closer to the beach and Main Street, denser and more walkable, $/sqft ~$1,875. Sunset Park sits east — quieter streets, ~15–20% lower $/sqft, more space and yard. For families prioritizing space and schools over beach-walkability, Sunset Park typically wins.

What is the Santa Monica Airport conversion?

The airport — Sunset Park's eastern boundary — is being converted to a regional "Great Park." That has driven measurable appreciation: 90405 values up ~12% since early 2025, with the transition cited as a key catalyst. As the park completes, the eastern blocks gain a major open-space amenity.
