Sunset Park: Santa Monica's Hidden Gem

Why this underpriced Santa Monica neighborhood is quietly attracting some of the most discerning buyers on the Westside — and what that means for value-seekers today.

Nestled in the southern part of Santa Monica, Sunset Park is a charming neighborhood that perfectly balances laid-back living with the convenience of urban amenities. Known for its tree-lined streets and close-knit community, Sunset Park offers residents a serene escape while remaining close to everything Santa Monica provides.

A Prime Location with Easy Access

Sunset Park’s ideal location is one of its biggest draws. Bordered by Pico Boulevard to the north, Lincoln Boulevard to the west, Venice to the south, and the Santa Monica Airport to the east, the neighborhood provides easy access to the beach and downtown Santa Monica. Its proximity to nearby freeways also makes commuting to other parts of Los Angeles convenient, adding to the neighborhood’s appeal for those seeking both tranquility and accessibility.

An Eclectic Mix of Homes

Sunset Park's architectural landscape is as diverse as its community. From classic California bungalows and mid-century homes to contemporary builds, the variety of housing styles adds to the neighborhood’s unique charm. The mix of single-family homes, duplexes, and small apartment buildings caters to a range of residents, from young professionals to growing families.

A Relaxed, Family-Friendly Atmosphere

With its strong sense of community, Sunset Park is a neighborhood where residents genuinely feel at home. The area is known for its family-friendly environment, bolstered by numerous parks, schools, and local amenities. Clover Park, a community favorite, offers expansive green spaces, sports fields, playgrounds, and picnic areas, making it a perfect spot for families and outdoor enthusiasts.

Dining and Shopping with Local Flair

Though primarily residential, Sunset Park boasts a variety of dining and shopping options. Ocean Park Boulevard, one of the neighborhood’s main streets, is lined with cozy cafes, upscale restaurants, boutiques, fitness studios, and specialty stores. The nearby Santa Monica Farmers Market provides fresh produce and local goods, adding to the area’s charm and convenience.

A Strong Sense of Community

Sunset Park is more than just a place to live; it’s a community where neighbors know each other and local events foster connections. Active neighborhood groups and community events testify to the strong bonds that define this area, offering residents a true sense of belonging and camaraderie.

Education and Schools

Education is a priority in Sunset Park, with access to some of Santa Monica’s top public schools. The neighborhood is home to Santa Monica College’s main campus, contributing to its youthful and vibrant atmosphere. Local schools such as John Adams Middle School and Santa Monica High School are well-regarded, making the neighborhood an attractive option for families.

Why Sunset Park Stands Out

Sunset Park is a hidden gem within Santa Monica—a neighborhood that offers the best of both worlds. With its relaxed vibe, strong community spirit, and convenient access to urban amenities, Sunset Park is a place where residents can enjoy a quieter lifestyle without sacrificing proximity to the vibrant culture and opportunities of Santa Monica.

For those looking to discover a unique and welcoming community within the bustling city of Santa Monica, Sunset Park is a neighborhood that truly feels like home.

The Sunset Park Real Estate Market in 2026.

The headline number that most people quote about Sunset Park — a median sale price of roughly $2.5 million as of early 2026, up about 43% year-over-year — is real but partially a composition story. Larger and more thoroughly renovated homes have transacted at higher rates, pulling the median up. The fuller 12-month average sits closer to $2.0 million. Median price per square foot is approximately $1,550. Homes typically sell in 38–41 days, which is faster than the broader Santa Monica market.

The structural drivers of recent price action are unusually clear. First: post-fire displacement demand from buyers seeking flat-lot, lower-density Santa Monica away from the hillside has concentrated heavily in the 90405 ZIP code, where Sunset Park sits. Second: the Santa Monica Airport conversion to a regional park ("Great Park") has added a major open-space amenity directly adjacent to the neighborhood — measured at roughly 12% price appreciation in 90405 since early 2025 alone, with the airport-park transition cited as a key catalyst.

What You Buy in Sunset Park Santa Monica by Price Band (mid-2026)
The sweet spot is $2M–$2.75M. That band is where most of the post-fire displacement demand has landed: updated 3-bedroom homes on 6,000–7,000 square foot lots, walkable to Clover Park and the future airport park, in the Will Rogers / Grant Elementary boundary.

The Sub-Areas of Sunset Park.

Locals divide Sunset Park into recognizable pockets that price and trade differently. Worth understanding before you tour:

North of Ocean Park Boulevard (closer to Pico)

The northernmost strip — between Pico and Ocean Park Boulevard — is generally the most affordable part of Sunset Park. More mixed housing stock, some multifamily, less consistent restoration. Walkable to Pico Boulevard's commercial spine. Real entry-point opportunity for buyers who can accept the trade-off in immediate walkability to the airport-park.

Between Ocean Park Boulevard and Pearl Street

The most consistently desirable middle band of Sunset Park. Quiet residential streets — 14th, 16th, 18th, 20th — known for tree-lined character, intact Douglas-era housing well-restored, and a tight-knit neighborhood feel. This is where the bulk of $2M–$2.75M family transactions happen. Walking distance to both Clover Park and the future airport-park entrance.

South of Pearl toward Marine Street

The southern reach of Sunset Park trades slightly higher per square foot. Closer to Marine Street's commercial pocket, the Marina/Venice border, and access into Mar Vista. Some of the most-renovated examples of the Douglas-era housing stock live on these blocks — restored Spanish Revival, Tudor, and mid-century homes that helped redefine the neighborhood's character over the past decade.

Eastern Edge (Clover Park / 23rd-25th Streets)

The east-side blocks abutting Clover Park and the future airport-park land are arguably the strongest appreciation pocket of Sunset Park in 2026. Park-adjacent inventory has historically commanded a premium in Santa Monica, and as the airport-park conversion completes, this band may continue to outperform the broader Sunset Park average.

Schools — The SMMUSD Calculus.

Sunset Park is part of Santa Monica-Malibu Unified School District. The two elementary assignments matter:

  • Will Rogers Learning Community (PreK–5). Often referred to simply as Will Rogers. One of SMMUSD's most-requested elementaries with a strong reputation for academic performance and a tight parent community. Boundary covers a significant portion of central and eastern Sunset Park.
  • Grant Elementary (K–5). Also highly rated. Boundary covers more of western Sunset Park near Lincoln. The neighborhood line between Will Rogers and Grant runs through the middle of Sunset Park — verify by exact address with SMMUSD.
  • John Adams Middle School (6–8). Feeder middle school for the area. Strong magnet programs.
  • Santa Monica High School (Samohi). The neighborhood's high school, with career and technical education pathways in engineering, computer science, automotive, and film.

The school comparison most buyers actually make is Sunset Park schools vs. North of Montana schools. North of Montana feeds into Roosevelt or Franklin Elementary, which carry SMMUSD's strongest rankings and the corresponding ~6% home-price premium per UCLA-area research. For families who want the SMMUSD district without the $4M+ North of Montana entry point, Sunset Park is the standard answer — Will Rogers and Grant are genuinely strong, and the price gap is dramatic.

A note for ADU-curious buyers

Sunset Park has one of the highest concentrations of R2-zoned lots in Santa Monica — meaning many parcels permit a guest house or accessory dwelling unit (ADU). For buyers thinking about value-add through a back-house rental or multi-generational living, this matters. Confirm the specific zoning of any prospective lot with the Santa Monica Planning Department before writing an offer.

Sunset Park vs. North of Montana vs. Ocean Park.

The three honest comparisons most Santa Monica buyers face:

  • vs. North of Montana (90402): NoMo prices roughly $3.85M+ median for single-family — nearly double Sunset Park — and feeds the most prestigious SMMUSD elementaries (Roosevelt, Franklin). Sunset Park trades the prestige and the absolute-top schools for meaningfully better square-foot-per-dollar and the same SMMUSD district.
  • vs. Ocean Park (also 90405): Ocean Park sits west of Lincoln, closer to the beach and Main Street, with extreme walkability (Walk Score 90+) and average $/sqft near $1,875. Sunset Park trades the walkable density for more space, more yard, and quieter streets.
  • vs. Mar Vista / Venice: Sunset Park's southern blocks blur into the Mar Vista / Marina border, and inventory crosses freely. For buyers looking specifically for SMMUSD schools, Sunset Park wins; for buyers indifferent to district lines, Mar Vista often prices 10–15% lower for similar housing stock.

The Honest Trade-offs.

  • Airport noise (for now). The Santa Monica Airport is still operational pending full conversion. The eastern blocks of Sunset Park hear it. The conversion to a regional park is in progress, but timing on full closure has shifted multiple times — price in the current operational reality, plan for the long-term upside.
  • Compact lots compared to NoMo. 6,000–8,000 square foot lots are the norm. That's generous by central LA standards but modest compared to North of Montana's larger parcels.
  • Restoration risk on the Douglas-era stock. Many homes still date from the 1940s. Original-condition listings can present meaningfully higher rehab costs than buyers anticipate, particularly for plumbing, electrical, and seismic retrofit work.
  • Heat without ocean breeze on the eastern edge. Sunset Park is far enough from the beach that the strongest marine layer cooling thins out by mid-day in summer. AC matters, even in Santa Monica.

The Right Frame for a Sunset Park Search.

Sunset Park is one of the best risk/reward neighborhoods in Santa Monica for buyers who prioritize SMMUSD schools, walkable family infrastructure, and modest-but-restorable mid-century housing — without paying the $4M+ entry point of the North of Montana area. The buyers who do best here get specific about three things up front: the elementary boundary their target address falls into, the sub-area trade-offs (Pearl vs. Pico vs. Marine), and the realistic restoration math on Douglas-era homes. Get those three right, and Sunset Park delivers Santa Monica quality of life at a meaningfully lower-than-Westside price.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where is Sunset Park in Santa Monica?

Sunset Park sits in the southern portion of Santa Monica, bounded by Pico Boulevard (north), Lincoln Boulevard (west, separating it from Ocean Park), Venice/Marine Street (south), and the Santa Monica Airport (east). It's primarily within ZIP 90405, originally developed in the 1940s for Douglas Aircraft workers.

What is the median home price in Sunset Park 2026?

The median home sale price is approximately $2.5 million (early 2026), up ~43% YoY. The 12-month average sits closer to $2.0M. Median $/sqft is ~$1,550. Homes sell in 38–41 days. Most single-family transactions fall $1.8M–$3M.

What schools serve Sunset Park?

SMMUSD (Santa Monica-Malibu Unified). Two elementary assignments: Will Rogers Learning Community or Grant Elementary depending on exact block. Feeds into John Adams Middle School and Santa Monica High School (Samohi). Verify by exact address with SMMUSD — the Will Rogers / Grant boundary runs through the neighborhood.

How is Sunset Park different from Ocean Park?

Adjacent, separated by Lincoln Blvd. Ocean Park sits west, closer to the beach and Main Street, denser and more walkable, $/sqft ~$1,875. Sunset Park sits east, quieter residential streets, ~15–20% lower $/sqft, more space and yard. For families prioritizing space and schools over beach-walkability, Sunset Park typically wins.

What is the Santa Monica Airport conversion?

The Santa Monica Airport (Sunset Park's eastern boundary) is being converted to a regional "Great Park." This has driven measurable appreciation — 90405 property values up ~12% since early 2025 with the airport-park transition cited as a key catalyst. As the park completes, Sunset Park's eastern blocks gain a major open-space amenity adjacent to the neighborhood.
