Buying a Duplex in Los Angeles — how the math works.

Owner-occupying a duplex lets you use primary-residence financing while a tenant helps pay your mortgage — a combination that's hard to replicate with any other real estate strategy.

Michael Abraham
Principal Agent, AMRE Real Estate Group · May 20, 2026 · 6 min read

Buying a duplex in Los Angeles is the most under-appreciated wealth-building move available to an owner-occupant. Live in one unit, rent the other, and let a tenant carry a meaningful share of your mortgage while you build equity on the whole building. To make it concrete, take a real example: 1423 W 65th St, Los Angeles, CA 90047, a fully remodeled duplex listed at $799,900.

  • New roof and fully paid-off solar panels, reducing utility costs.

Why the math works.

The core idea is simple: your real housing cost is the mortgage minus the rent you collect. Renting the second unit for about $2,000 per month reduces the net monthly payment on this property to roughly $3,131.86 — the difference between a full payment and a manageable net cost of ownership. That's what makes homeownership pencil in a market as expensive as Los Angeles.

The 10-year picture.

Los Angeles real estate has historically appreciated steadily. If 1423 W 65th St appreciates at 7% annually, its value could nearly double — to roughly $1,573,130 in ten years. Meanwhile, renting one unit at $2,000 per month generates up to $240,000 in rental income over the decade, helping cover the mortgage and lifting your return.

Owner-occupy a duplex and a tenant effectively pays a large share of your mortgage while you build equity on the entire building.

Put together, holding this duplex for ten years could produce a net gain of over $1,023,671 and an ROI around 581.5%. Despite initial negative cash-on-cash returns from a high mortgage payment, equity growth and appreciation drive strong long-term results — an estimated 10–12% IRR over the period. Key takeaways

  • Dual income: a tenant's rent offsets your mortgage and cushions unexpected costs.
  • Lower entry: owner-occupant financing beats investor loans on rate and down payment.
  • Appreciation + paydown: equity builds on the whole building, not just your unit.
  • Beginner-friendly: a duplex is easier to finance than a larger multifamily building.

Frequently asked questions.

Can you live in one unit of a duplex and rent the other in LA?

Yes — it's the core owner-occupant strategy, often called house-hacking. You live in one unit as your primary residence and rent the second to a tenant whose rent offsets much of your mortgage. Because you occupy the property, a 2–4 unit building still qualifies for owner-occupant financing.

How much can renting one unit cut your mortgage?

The offset is substantial. In our worked example, renting the second unit for about $2,000 per month reduces the net monthly payment to roughly $3,131.86 — turning a full payment into a far more manageable net cost.

Is a duplex a good first investment in LA?

For many first-time investors, yes. A duplex is typically more affordable and easier to finance than larger multifamily, gives you a place to live plus rental income, and lets appreciation and tenant-funded paydown build equity — a lower-friction entry into the Los Angeles market.

What financing can I use for an owner-occupied duplex?

Because you occupy one unit, owner-occupant programs apply — FHA and conventional owner-occupant loans — generally with lower down payments and better rates than investor financing. Projected rental income from the second unit can also help you qualify. Confirm current terms with a lender.
