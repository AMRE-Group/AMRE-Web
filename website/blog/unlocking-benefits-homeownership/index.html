Why Is Homeownership Important? Six Benefits That Compound.

From building equity to tax advantages, ownership consistently outperforms renting over the long term — the American Housing Survey data makes a compelling case. Here's what it means for buyers in Los Angeles.

Michael Abraham
Principal Agent, Architect · Apr 29, 2026 · 4 min read

Dreaming of owning a home — or wondering whether it's the right decision for your lifestyle and financial future? The latest American Housing Survey data reveals compelling, measurable reasons why owning tends to win over time. Here are the six that matter most.

1. More space to call your own.

Homeowners enjoy a median home size of 1,900 square feet — more than double the median 849 square feet renters occupy. A home office, a yard for kids and pets, real storage: space changes what your daily life can hold.

2. Stability and community.

Owners stay put longer than renters — which means stronger neighbor relationships, deeper local roots, and more stability for your family.

3. Equity: the built-in savings plan.

Every mortgage payment builds equity — savings that grow as you pay down the loan and the home appreciates. The median household income for homeowners is $78,000, nearly double renters' $41,000 — a gap that reflects the long-term wealth-building effect of ownership. 4. Predictable payments, tax advantages.

With a fixed-rate mortgage, principal and interest stay constant — unlike rent, which resets upward annually. Owners may also deduct mortgage interest and property taxes. Choosing the right loan matters as much as the house; our LA mortgage types guide walks through the options.

5. Freedom to personalize.

Paint boldly, plant a garden, add an ADU — ownership gives you creative control renters never get.

6. Control over your living environment.

No landlord, no surprise rent hikes, no lease-renewal anxiety. Your home is yours for as long as you want it.

Ownership converts your biggest monthly expense into your biggest long-term asset.

Is it the right move for you?

It depends on your finances, lifestyle, and horizon. If you plan to stay five-plus years and can manage the down payment, the compounding usually favors buying.
