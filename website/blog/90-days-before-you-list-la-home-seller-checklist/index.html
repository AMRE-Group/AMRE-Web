The 90 days before you list: an LA seller's checklist.

Days 90–60: the decisions that affect your net.

Pull your permit history.

Unpermitted work is one of the most common — and most expensive — surprises in an LA transaction. Pull your own records through the city or county portal before a buyer's inspector does. Unpermitted square footage, a garage conversion, an ADU without permits: your options are disclose and price accordingly, permit retroactively, or convert back. None are fast decisions. You need 90 days to handle this properly.

Order a preliminary title report.

Liens, clouds on title, and easement issues surface here. A pre-listing title review costs roughly $150–200 and can save weeks of escrow delays. If there's anything unusual, you want to know before buyers do.

Decide on Compass Concierge or your own contractor.

If your home needs work — kitchen refresh, paint, landscaping, flooring — you have about 75 days from this point to finish before photography. Compass Concierge funds eligible improvements upfront with no interest, repaid at closing. Going the independent route? Get bids now — good LA contractors are booked weeks out.

Get a pre-listing appraisal on higher-value homes.

For homes likely to attract jumbo financing, a pre-listing appraisal sets realistic expectations and arms you with documentation if a buyer's appraisal comes in low. Almost always useful above $2 million.

Days 60–30: preparation and positioning.

Complete all deferred maintenance.

Sounds obvious; almost never gets done properly. The checklist: HVAC service and tune-up if due, roof inspection and minor repairs, water heater age check (buyers and lenders flag units over 10 years), exterior caulking and paint touch-ups, and a full check of GFCIs, smoke detectors, and CO detectors — California law requires working smoke and CO detectors at transfer, a $15 item that shows up on every inspection report if missed.

Run your NHD report.

Natural Hazard Disclosure reports are required in California and cover fire zones, flood zones, and earthquake fault proximity. LA County has expanded high-fire-hazard zones significantly over the past five years — know your zone before a buyer sees it in the report.

Begin the staging conversation.

Good LA stagers are booked 3–4 weeks out for occupied homes, longer for vacant ones. Staged homes here consistently sell faster and higher than unstaged equivalents — the ROI is reliably positive, typically $3–5 returned per $1 spent.

Audit your HOA, if applicable.

Pull current financials, 12 months of meeting minutes, and any pending special assessments. Surprises — a large assessment, active litigation, reserves below 10% — kill deals or force price reductions. Better to know now.

Days 30–0: the launch.

Professional photography is non-negotiable.

Roughly 97% of buyers start online — the photos are the first showing. Budget for stills, video walkthrough, and 3D Matterport above $1.5M or for complex layouts. Drone photography earns its cost on view lots and notable outdoor space.

The Private Exclusive window.

Before public MLS launch, Compass's Private Exclusive program markets your home to 34,000+ Compass agents — generating early interest, gathering price-sensitivity intelligence, and creating urgency before days-on-market starts counting. Typically 1–2 weeks, and worth using on virtually every LA listing.

Price with data, not sentiment.

The most expensive mistake sellers make is pricing to what they want rather than what the data supports. An overpriced LA listing typically sits 30+ days, accumulates a "stale" signal, and closes below where a correctly priced listing would have — with more stress. Your agent should provide a written CMA with genuine comparables, not a number designed to win the listing. What you keep matters more than what you list at: see the five factors that drive your net proceeds.

Time the market week, not just the season.

In LA, Thursday is the strongest day to go live — buyers search over the weekend and your listing has maximum freshness for Saturday showings. Spring (February–May) sees the highest activity, but well-priced inventory moves in every season.

Top-dollar results don't happen by accident. They happen because a seller made good decisions 90 days before anyone saw the home.

Key takeaways

  • Start 90 days out: permits, title, and renovation decisions can't be rushed.
  • Fix what every inspection flags — detectors, water heater, roof, HVAC — before buyers find it.
  • Stage it and shoot it professionally: 97% of buyers meet your home online first.
  • Use the Private Exclusive window, price to the data, and launch on a Thursday.

FAQ: preparing to sell in Los Angeles.

How long before listing should I start preparing?

About 90 days. Permit research, title review, contractor or Concierge work, deferred maintenance, staging, and pre-marketing each take weeks — and several must happen in sequence. Compressing preparation into two weeks routinely leaves money on the table.

What should I fix before selling a house in LA?

Handle what shows up on every inspection: HVAC service, roof repairs, water heaters over 10 years old, exterior touch-ups, and working smoke and CO detectors (required in California at transfer). Resolve or disclose unpermitted work, and run your NHD report early.

Is professional staging worth it?

Reliably yes at most price points — staged LA homes sell faster and higher, with a typical return of $3–5 per $1 spent. Book 3–4 weeks ahead.

What is the best day to list a home in LA?

Thursday — buyers search over the weekend, so a Thursday launch has maximum freshness for Saturday showings. Spring sees the most activity, but well-priced homes move year-round.

What is the Compass Private Exclusive window?

A 1–2 week pre-MLS phase where your home is marketed to 34,000+ Compass agents — early interest, price intelligence, and urgency before days-on-market starts counting.
