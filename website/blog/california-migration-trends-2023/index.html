Where Are Californians Moving? The 2023 Data.

422,075 moved in. 690,502 moved out. And 89% of Californians who moved never left the state at all. The real story behind the migration headlines — and what it means for Los Angeles real estate.

Michael Abraham
Principal Agent, Architect · Feb 21, 2026 · 4 min read

California's migration in 2023 was marked by robust movement in both directions. Over 422,000 residents moved to California from other states while nearly 690,000 left — yet the least-reported number is the most important one: 89% of Californians who moved stayed within the state.

Where are Californians moving to?

The top destinations for the 690,502 Californians who left in 2023 read like a map of lower-cost, lower-tax neighbors. Texas received the most by a wide margin, with Arizona and Nevada — both an easy drive back — close behind.

Who's moving in?

California remains a magnet: 422,075 people arrived from other states in 2023. The biggest feeder states mirror the outbound list — evidence of genuine two-way traffic rather than a one-way exodus.

Why does Texas top both lists?

Texas received 93,970 Californians and sent back 38,732 of its own. Job relocations, family ties, and housing-cost arbitrage all play a part — and the two states' economies are deeply linked. Migration between them is less an exodus than a circulation.

The 89% that headlines miss.

Nearly nine in ten Californians who moved in 2023 relocated within California — chasing job changes, lifestyle shifts, and more affordable pockets of the state. That churn is exactly what we see on the ground in Los Angeles: households trading neighborhoods rather than states, which keeps demand resilient in emerging areas. It's a dynamic we track closely in pieces like how Long Beach is changing LA and the market's quiet comeback.

Key takeaways

  • Net domestic out-migration was ~268K in 2023 — real, but far smaller than the gross numbers suggest.
  • Texas dominates both directions: 93,970 out, 38,732 in.
  • 89% of California movers stayed in state — intra-state demand is the underrated story.
  • For LA buyers, migration churn creates opportunity in repricing neighborhoods.
