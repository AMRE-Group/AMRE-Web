Searching for a beachside home under $1 million in Los Angeles can be challenging — especially in popular Westside neighborhoods like Santa Monica, Brentwood, West LA, and Westwood. After visiting more than 30 properties across these areas, the picture is clear: at this budget you're buying beach-adjacent, not beachfront, and each neighborhood asks for a different trade-off between price, space, and lifestyle.

South Santa Monica — coastal character.

Vibe: laid-back beach town. Range: $700K–$1.0M. Types: bungalows and small apartment buildings, many dating to the 1960s–80s. You get real beach proximity and a vibrant, walkable neighborhood with long-term appreciation potential — in exchange for older buildings that may need work, more tourism-driven noise, and tight parking. Tip: look for renovation potential; a little investment goes a long way in a prime location.

North Santa Monica — upscale coastal.

Vibe: polished and sophisticated. Range: $900K–$1.2M+. Types: condos, townhomes, some single-family. North of Lincoln you'll find well-maintained properties, tree-lined streets, top schools, and upscale shopping — at a higher price that often exceeds $1M. Tip: focus just outside the main tourist areas for the best balance of luxury and calm.

Brentwood — suburban oasis.

Vibe: relaxed affluence. Range: $800K–$1.1M. Types: condos, townhomes, smaller single-family homes. San Vicente Boulevard anchors a family-friendly neighborhood with excellent schools and parks and easy access to both beach and city — though single-family homes run higher and it can feel a step removed from the sand. Tip: hunt near the Brentwood Country Mart for charm plus convenience.

West LA — affordable urban energy.

Vibe: busy, diverse, convenient. Range: $600K–$900K. Types: apartments, condos, some townhomes. Between Santa Monica and Olympic, West LA is the value play — more affordable, great dining, and quick freeway access, with more traffic and less of a beachy feel as the cost. Tip: buy near the Santa Monica or Brentwood borders to capture neighborhood appeal at a lower price.

Westwood — quiet elegance near UCLA.

Vibe: academic and residential. Range: $700K–$1.0M. Types: condos, some single-family. Slightly hilly, tree-lined streets and proximity to UCLA make Westwood a serene middle ground between city and suburb; the student population can shape the rental market and event-day traffic. Tip: North Westwood Village blends academic charm with residential calm.

At this budget you're not choosing a beach house — you're choosing which trade-off you can live with: location, space, condition, or commute.

Making the right trade-offs.

Finding your ideal beach-adjacent property under $1M is an exercise in thoughtful compromise. The four levers that move together:

01

Location vs. space

Closer to the beach usually means a smaller unit. Decide which you'll defend.

02

Condition vs. price

Fixer-uppers offer value but need capital and time — budget for both.

03

Energy vs. quiet

Vibrant, walkable blocks come with more noise, traffic, and parking pressure.

Pro tips for savvy buyers

  • Explore in person — walk each neighborhood at different times of day.
  • Research upcoming development that could shift future values.
  • Check HOA fees and reserves; they can swing your monthly cost.
  • Get pre-approved so you can move fast in a competitive segment.
  • Work with a local expert who can surface off-market options.
