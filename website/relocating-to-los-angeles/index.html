The Bigger Picture

Not just a city. One of the world's great economies.

People move to LA for the light and the lifestyle — but they stay because it's one of the most diversified economies on earth. Entertainment, tech, aerospace, design, trade, and health care all anchor here, which is exactly why the housing market has held its value through every cycle.

The five-county LA region produces well over a trillion dollars a year — an output that would rank among the largest national economies in the world if it stood alone. It's home to the busiest port complex in the Western Hemisphere, the global center of film and television, and a fast-growing "Silicon Beach" tech corridor along the Westside.

For a buyer, that diversity is downside protection: no single industry can sink the whole market. For an agent relocating a client, it's the simplest pitch there is — the jobs, the capital, and the culture are all already here.

Business & Industry

The companies moving here.

Culver City has quietly become one of the country's hottest corporate addresses. Apple built out a major campus at the Culver Steps for its Apple TV+ and video teams; Amazon Studios is headquartered a few blocks away at the historic Culver Studios; and TikTok, HBO, and Beats all took large footprints nearby — all in the shadow of Sony Pictures' historic Culver City lot.

Along the coast, "Silicon Beach" keeps pulling talent: Google's LA campus in the Playa Vista Spruce Goose hangar, Snap headquartered in Santa Monica, and SpaceX anchoring aerospace in nearby Hawthorne. New payrolls like these are exactly what drive housing demand — and the relocations we help with every week.

Culture

Two landmark museums, brand new.

LA's cultural map is being redrawn in real time. Two of the most anticipated museum projects in the country just opened within a few miles of each other.

Miracle Mile · Open now

LACMA's new front door

Peter Zumthor's David Geffen Galleries opened on the Miracle Mile in April 2026 — a sweeping, single-level concrete ribbon that finally gives the largest art museum in the western U.S. the building it deserves.

It anchors a Museum Row that already includes the Academy Museum, the La Brea Tar Pits, and Chris Burden's beloved Urban Light — turning Mid-Wilshire into one of the most walkable cultural corridors in the city.

LACMA · Miracle Mile
Exposition Park · Open now

The Lucas Museum

The billion-dollar Lucas Museum of Narrative Art — a curving, cloud-like showpiece by MAD Architects — anchors Exposition Park, alongside the Coliseum, the Science Center, and the Natural History Museum.

It's one of the most ambitious cultural buildings ever built in Los Angeles, and a gravitational pull for South LA's continued renaissance.

Getting Around

The car capital is learning to ride the rail.

Two Metro projects are quietly rewriting how you move across the Westside — and where it makes sense to buy.

D Line · The Purple Line

The subway heads west

Metro is tunneling the D Line under Wilshire toward Century City and Westwood — connecting downtown to the Westside's biggest job centers by subway for the first time. Phased openings are targeted before the 2028 Games.

E Line · To the sand

Light rail to the Pier

The E Line already drops you two blocks from the Santa Monica Pier — a one-seat ride from downtown to the ocean, reshaping neighborhoods all along the corridor from Culver City to the coast.

Street of the Moment
Abbot Kinney, Venice

"The coolest block in America."

GQ famously crowned Abbot Kinney Boulevard the coolest block in America — and a decade on, it still sets the tone for the Westside.

A mile of independent boutiques, coffee bars, galleries, and restaurants, ending two blocks from the beach and the Venice canals. It's the clearest shorthand there is for the way LA blends design, food, and coastal ease into a single afternoon.

For a relocating buyer, streets like this are the sell — the walkable, one-of-a-kind neighborhoods you simply can't find in most of the country.

Development & Policy

The backyard unit that finally pencils.

California rewrote the rules on accessory dwelling units — and it changed the math on owning here. New state law lets homeowners build a backyard unit and actually sell it separately, with no requirement to live on-site.

For a buyer, that turns a single lot into two potential income streams or a sellable asset. For an investor, it's one of the clearest value-add plays in the state — a big part of why LA real estate keeps working even at today's prices.

Relocation FAQ

Moving to LA, answered.

Why are people moving to Los Angeles right now?

New museums like LACMA's David Geffen Galleries and the Lucas Museum, major corporate campuses in Culver City and Silicon Beach, an expanding Metro rail network, and the 2026 World Cup, 2027 Super Bowl and 2028 Olympics — on top of the weather, beaches, universities and food.

Is Los Angeles a good place to relocate for work?

Yes. Los Angeles has one of the world's most diversified economies — entertainment, tech, aerospace, trade and health care — with Apple, Amazon Studios, Google, Snap and SpaceX all expanding across the region.
