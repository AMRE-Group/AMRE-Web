As Featured in the Los Angeles Times

Bringing Smiles

A short walk from the front door, a 100-year-old carob tree is one of the neighborhood's quiet landmarks. When its roots began buckling the sidewalk, a resident answered not with hazard cones but with whimsy — building a tiny fairy-and-gnome world into the trunk from popsicle-stick doors, pine-cone shingles, and miniature walkways. It draws photographs, fan mail, and small handmade gifts from neighborhood children, and it became a Los Angeles Times story about how a little magic can make strangers smile. Move in, and the tradition of bringing smiles is yours to continue.
