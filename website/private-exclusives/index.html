The off-market reality

A quiet market runs alongside the public one.

In Los Angeles, the homes most likely to trade — at the top of the market and across architecturally significant inventory — often never reach Zillow, Redfin, or the MLS. They move privately, through Compass Private Exclusives, agent-to-agent pocket networks, and pre-market arrangements weeks or months before any public listing.

If you're searching only on public sites, you're seeing a fraction of what's actually available. The Private Access List exists to close that gap — so you see real opportunities in real time, before they hit the open market.

What it is

Compass Private Exclusives, explained.

A Compass Private Exclusive is an off-market home listing that's only shared inside the Compass agent network — never published to Zillow, Redfin, or the public MLS. Sellers use it to test pricing, protect their privacy, control market timing, or quietly find the right buyer before any public exposure.

At any given moment, Compass agents have visibility into several hundred Private Exclusive listings across Los Angeles. Add to that the pocket listings circulating outside Compass — and the pre-market homes being prepped for launch — and the off-market layer rivals or exceeds the public MLS for trophy inventory.

The catch: you can't see it from the outside. You need a buyer's agent inside that network, with a daily habit of working it.

Why it matters

Four reasons sellers go off-market.

Understanding seller motivation is how buyers find the right entry point. Each scenario produces a different negotiation dynamic.

01

Discretion

High-profile owners, sensitive timing — divorce, estate, career transitions — or properties where the seller simply doesn't want neighbors and friends seeing their home on Zillow. Privacy is the product.

02

Price discovery

Sellers testing a number before public exposure. If a price doesn't hold privately, they recalibrate — without a public price drop visible to every buyer pool forever.

03

Market protection

Days-on-market metrics on the public MLS punish properties that don't sell quickly. Going off-market first lets a seller find the right buyer without burning down the listing.

04

Buyer quality

A private process signals serious, qualified buyers only. Sellers (and their agents) often prefer the controlled deal flow of the off-market layer over the open-market chaos of weekends and Zillow inquiries.

For the buyer

Why off-market access changes the search.

When you see homes before the rest of the market does, three things change at once — and they compound.

01

Less competition

The bidding war happens on the public MLS, with thirty other buyers and a weekend deadline. Off-market, you're often the only serious party at the table — or one of two or three.

02

More time to think

No artificial deadlines. You can walk the property twice, bring a contractor, sleep on it, and run the numbers properly — without the pressure of a Tuesday best-and-final.

03

Real conversations

Without a public listing churning, the agent-to-agent dialogue stays substantive. We can ask hard questions, negotiate creative terms, and structure deals public-market urgency would never allow.

04

Better deal economics

Off-market doesn't automatically mean a discount — but it does mean room to negotiate. Without ten competing offers, the leverage equation flips back toward the buyer who shows up prepared and serious.

Worth knowing

Off-market in LA — six things to know.

i.

The off-market layer is large.

Industry estimates suggest that in trophy LA markets (Beverly Hills, Bel Air, Holmby Hills, Malibu), 25–40% of luxury transactions involve off-market or pre-market activity at some stage.

ii.

Compass leads in private inventory.

The Compass Private Exclusives platform is the largest formal off-market network in residential real estate, with hundreds of LA listings active at any time — only visible to Compass agents and their clients.

iii.

Pocket listings are still common.

Outside Compass, many top LA agents at other brokerages still quietly represent homes off-market through agent networks, broker tours, and direct referral channels — separate from Private Exclusives.

iv.

Architecturally significant homes trade quietly.

Schindler, Neutra, Lautner, and Buff & Hensman homes — and contemporary works by name architects — disproportionately trade off-market, often before any photography is shot.

v.

Off-market doesn't mean discounted.

The right framing is access, not bargain. Off-market homes often trade at strong numbers — but the buyer with access gets to compete on terms and timing, not just price under public pressure.

vi.

You need an agent inside the network.

Off-market access doesn't come from a website or an app. It comes from the daily work of an agent inside the broker network — relationships, tours, calls, and the willingness to source properties that don't exist publicly.

Off-market in Los Angeles — questions buyers ask.

What is a Compass Private Exclusive?

A Compass Private Exclusive is an off-market home listing that is only shared inside the Compass agent network — never published to Zillow, Redfin, or the public MLS. Sellers use Private Exclusives to test pricing, protect their privacy, or quietly find the right buyer before going public. There are typically several hundred Private Exclusive listings active across Los Angeles at any given time.

What is the difference between a pocket listing and an off-market listing?

A pocket listing is a home an individual agent is quietly representing without putting it on the MLS. An off-market listing is the broader term — it includes pocket listings, Compass Private Exclusives, pre-market homes being prepared for launch, and any home a seller is willing to sell but hasn't formally listed. In Los Angeles, a meaningful share of trophy inventory trades off-market each year.

How do I see off-market homes for sale in Los Angeles?

You need a buyer's agent with deep access to the Compass network and a strong local pocket-listing pipeline. At AMRE Real Estate Group, buyers join the Private Access List and receive a curated weekly brief of off-market homes that match their criteria — including Compass Private Exclusives, pocket listings from outside brokerages, and pre-market opportunities we source through our network.

Why do sellers list their homes off-market?

Sellers go off-market for four common reasons: discretion (high-profile owners, sensitive timing, divorce or estate situations), price discovery (testing a number before public exposure), market protection (avoiding "days on market" building up on Zillow if the price isn't right), and quality of buyer (signaling a private, qualified-buyer-only process). Compass Private Exclusives are designed specifically for these scenarios.

Is there a cost to access off-market listings?

No. In a standard buyer representation, the seller pays the buyer's agent commission at closing — there is no out-of-pocket cost to the buyer for representation or for joining AMRE's Private Access List. The list is free to join and you can opt out at any time.

How often does new off-market inventory hit the AMRE Private Access List?
